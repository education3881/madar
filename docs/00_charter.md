# Project Charter

## What this publication is

An editorial publication, automated end-to-end by five AI agents under a founder's direction, that surfaces high-quality writing and visuals about education in places that the international education conversation routinely under-serves.

We cover early childhood education (ECE) and K-12. We do not cover higher education.

## Why it exists

Three convictions:

1. **The international education conversation is geographically lopsided.** The Anglosphere talks to itself. Education stories from the Gulf, the Levant, West Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America circulate mostly when something goes wrong. We want to circulate them when something works, when something is being attempted, when something is contested.

2. **Editorial care signals respect.** Most coverage of education in these regions is functional — reports, white papers, press releases. There is room for a publication that approaches the same subjects with the visual and prose seriousness that the international press reserves for itself.

3. **AI automation, used well, can sustain a small editorial operation indefinitely.** A multi-agent system, supervised by one human, can publish at a sustainable cadence with quality controls a one-person blog cannot match.

## What this publication is not

- Not aggregation. Curation is editorial work, with framing, judgment, and voice.
- Not advocacy. We have a perspective (who we cover) but we don't tell readers what to conclude.
- Not a savior narrative. Local voices lead; outsider voices are clearly labeled as such.
- Not affiliated with any government, employer, or institution.

## Editorial principles

- **Name a place and a time.** Every piece is grounded in a specific country, district, school, or initiative, and a specific timeframe. No vague "in developing countries..."
- **Cite primary sources.** Ministry documents, named educators, published research, local reporting. If we can't cite, we don't publish.
- **Treat the reader as an adult.** No clickbait, no engagement bait, no hand-holding through obvious points.
- **Warmth without sentimentality.** It is possible to honor the dignity of the people in our stories without making them mascots.
- **Be useful to practitioners.** Educators, regulators, and parents should leave with something they can think with, not just feel about.

## What sustainable looks like

- Daily small drops (curated link + commentary, ≤300 words) — for quality assurance and a steady drumbeat.
- Weekly larger drops — one original essay (~1,500–2,500 words) + a curated digest.
- Monthly strategy reviews with the founder.
- Zero paid promotion in year one.

## What success looks like (year one)

Not page views. Not followers. Three things:

1. **Return readership.** A core audience that comes back every week — 200+ people would be a real success.
2. **Citation in serious places.** Practitioners, researchers, and journalists referencing our pieces.
3. **A complete archive.** ~150 published pieces across all in-scope geographies and themes by the end of the year.
