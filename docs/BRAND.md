# Brand

## Name

**Madār** (مدار)

Arabic for *orbit* / *place of return*. A genuine cross-script word, not a transliteration. It articulates the publication's geographic stance without ever stating it: we orbit the places others don't.

## Decided

2026-05-25, by founder, on Manager's recommendation, following Editor × Designer debate (recorded in [`../agents/debates/2026-05-25-brand-naming.md`](../agents/debates/2026-05-25-brand-naming.md)).

## Wordmark — production constraints (Designer)

- Custom-drawn wordmark, not a typeface-menu pick. Reference families for the Latin: Lyon, Newzald, Söhne Breit. For the Arabic: 29LT Zarid, TPTQ Naskh.
- Both scripts hold the same optical weight on the page. Arabic is never sized smaller or treated as an afterthought.
- The mark must hold at 8mm on a spine and at 400mm on a cover with no separate lockup.
- No icon. No tagline locked to the mark.
- The macron over the *a* (Madār) is the single typographic event the whole wordmark organizes around. Without it the word lies flat; with it, the mark has a focal point.

## Tagline (working)

> Education, from underrepresented geographies.

Note: per the Editor's pushback, "underrepresented" is on watch — it borrows NGO frame language we have said we'll avoid. A future iteration should name what we *do*, not what the rest of the press *fails* to do. Held for review until the seed content is in place and the publication's actual posture is visible.
