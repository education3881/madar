**Test 1 — Sourcing reality. PASS.** Every load-bearing URL re-verified live today (2026-06-16), not merely carried from the recon:
- Policy spine independently re-confirmed: Politburo decision 28 Feb 2025; National Assembly Resolution 217/2025/QH15 adopted 26 Jun 2025 (440/441) and certified by NA Chairman Trần Thanh Mẫn; ~VND 30,600 bn/year; public = full exemption, private/non-public = capped support. (Government Portal + VietnamPlus + VietnamNet.)

The piece attributes this **to Tiền Phong, in the principal's account — never in our own voice.** The aggregators the quote first surfaced in are not cited.
- **One correction made at this gate:** the UNICEF (Silvia Danailov) quote was tightened to the on-record wording — "an important step toward inclusion and equal opportunity for **all** children" — and the piece now also carries Danailov's own paired caution about textbooks, uniforms and transport, which strengthens the even-handedness (the residual-cost point is shared by the policy's champion, not imported by us).
- **Guardrails honoured:** both instruments named and kept distinct (Party decision vs. legislature's resolution); the single-stream / proposal-phase figures (VND 22,500 bn; VND 31.4 tn) are **kept out**; the "primary was already free / 5-year-olds free since Decree 81" nuance is in, so "free school" is not overclaimed; USD recomputed and **dated** at the mid-June 2026 rate (~VND 26,400/USD — materially stronger dong than the recon's 25,500; the ~US$1.2 bn and ~US$2–25/month figures survive the re-rate).

**Test 3 — Why now. PASS.** 2025–26 is the first school year under the exemption; the binding resolution was certified in June 2025. The reader knows by the first paragraph why this is in front of them now.

A senior Vietnamese educator would recognise the account as accurate and fair. The teacher-contribution detail is handled as the policy doing real work, not as dysfunction.
