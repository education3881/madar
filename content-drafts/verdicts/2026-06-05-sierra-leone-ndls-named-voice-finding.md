## The angle has moved — for the better

The original candidate was "a slow read of the NDLS strategy PDF." 1. **It closes the Bo loop precisely.** The anchor piece is a teacher in an under-resourced remote school. This is the system deciding *who gets sent to schools like that one, and how* — the same problem, viewed from the ministry. Field note → the machine that does the placing.

That connects directly to our Uruguay/Ceibal and broader LatAm coverage. A GCC-adjacent African story that also reaches back to Latin America is exactly the cross-regional reading Madār is for.
3. **It is a document/mechanism to read slowly** — transparent, data-driven, open-source — which is the house mode, not breaking news.

## Hard, citable specifics (verifiable, primary)

From the 2024 deployment round (TSC): **2,341 teachers** recruited; **100%** of selected teachers passed the licensing exam; **64%** deployed to schools with pupil-payroll-teacher ratio >100; **57%** to disadvantaged districts; **54%** matched to their preferred school; only **7%** placed more than 5 km from their preferred option. Tiebreakers include a gender-based rule favouring female teachers. Open-source code is public (`github.com/Fab-Inc/FabMatch_SLE`). Policy brief and technical reports carry DOIs.

The honest counter-texture is also on the record (this is not a press release): too few teachers passed the licensing exam to fill the pool; data-integration gaps between TMIS and the school census; limited TSC digital capacity; weak initial teacher communication. A piece that names both the gains and these limits will pass the five-test rubric on "does it tell the truth, including the inconvenient part."
