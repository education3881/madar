## What this piece is

Oman has stood up a national, external, standards-based school inspectorate (the OAAAQA "National System for Evaluating School Performance") and begun publishing per-school verdicts on a public portal — including naming one government school at the bottom band ("needs rapid intervention"). The spine is **not** "GCC state launches inspections" (a beat). It is the discipline that **the worth of an inspection regime is in what follows the verdict — support and appeal, or only exposure** — sharpened by the non-obvious timing: Oman has embraced the single public verdict at the exact moment its model, England's Ofsted, retreated from it (one-word judgements scrapped Sept 2024 after a coroner found an inspection contributed to a head teacher's death). The honest object is the fork Oman now stands at, not a grade on Oman.
