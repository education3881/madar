# Editor verdict — Brazil: "The literacy target, made national"

**Date:** 2026-06-25 · **Stage:** commission + approve, same run (recon banked 2026-06-24, source index `content-drafts/recon/2026-06-24-brazil-crianca-alfabetizada-source-index.md`)
**Verdict: APPROVE.** Ships EN + AR, Edition 02, `curated`. Sprint piece **8 of 10** — the first Edition-02 Latin-America piece, and only the corpus's second LatAm piece overall (after Uruguay).

## What this piece is

In June 2023 Brazil took Ceará's nearly-two-decade municipal literacy model (*Alfabetização na Idade Certa*, born in Sobral) and made it a **national compact** — the Compromisso Nacional Criança Alfabetizada — with a single annual number attached: the Indicador Criança Alfabetizada, the share of second-year public-school children reading and writing at the expected level. The number has risen three years running (56 → 59.2 → 66%, the 2025 figure announced March 2026, clearing the 64% target). The spine is **not** "Brazil's literacy is rising" (a beat). It is the **seam inside the headline number**: the Indicador is *administered by the same state systems it grades*, and set beside the federal external exam (Saeb), the two disagree by design — 56% vs 49.3% for 2023. The honest object is **who holds the ruler**, and what a system owes its readers when the grader and the graded are the same hand. This is a *made-measurable / made-national* story — the variety the reversal-heavy recent corpus (Korea, Philippines, Oman) needs.

## Figure gate (was CLOSED at recon) — re-verified live this run

Test 1 re-run (web tools, egress rule — not bash). Every load-bearing figure re-pulled to a named primary surface this run:
- **The series** 56% (2023) → 59.2% (2024) → 66% (2025), target 64%, 2030 goal 80% — Agência Gov (Mar 2026) + INEP `brasil_2025.pdf`. **20 of 27 federal units cleared the target** (19 states + DF) — INEP headline.
- **The seam (ruling #12, the piece's reason to exist):** state-administered Indicador **56%** vs externally-administered Saeb **49.3%** for 2023 — a 7-point gap the ministry acknowledged publicly ("the exam is not the indicator"). Agência Brasil (Apr 2025) + Terra/Folha. **New nuance verified and carried:** the gap is *not* "states marking their own homework" — the Indicador is a **census** (comparable school-to-school and state-to-state), while the Saeb is a **sample** whose per-state margin of error runs past 10 points; the same 743 cutoff (2023 *Alfabetiza Brasil*) underlies both. So the self-administered measure is, here, the *more comparable* one. The piece prints both numbers, attributes each, and names the gap as the finding — then refuses the cheap cynical read. **Comparability and independence are different virtues** (logged to the guidebook this run).
- **The decree** (Decreto 11.556, 12 Jun 2023; regime de colaboração; every state + DF + ~99.8% of ~5,570 municipalities) — Planalto + the live confirmation of the collaborative-regime framing.

## Voice gate (was substantially CLOSED) — seated at the system-operator tier

**PASS at the system-operator tier, not a route-2 gap-statement case.** Unlike Oman (06-20), where the only voice was the authority's CEO and the operational gap had to be stated in print, Brazil seats a genuine **operational** voice: **Eliana Estrela, Secretary of Education of Ceará**, datelined **March 2026**, runs the *state apparatus that is the national model* (Ceará's two-decade PAIC, Gold seal two years running). Her quote speaks to the *mechanism* ("the capillarity of our public policies… all Ceará children learn at the right age"), not a national announcement. That clears Test 2. **Camilo Santana (MEC) and Lula are deliberately NOT used as sourcing voices** (ruling #1 — announcement/ceremonial tiers are not operational). **Logged upgrade path** (not a blocking gap): a Sobral municipal literacy coordinator or a named second-year teacher on *how a child is got to standard by year-end* would lift this from system-operator to classroom tier — a buffer-day or future-edition follow.

## Five-test rubric

1. **Sourcing / falsifiability — PASS.** 9 sources, every figure re-verified live this run to a named primary surface (INEP PDF, Planalto decree, Seduc-Ceará, Agência Gov/Brasil, Terra, Todos Pela Educação). The seam figure (Saeb 49.3%) carried with its methodological caveat, not as a gotcha.
2. **Named operational voice — PASS (system-operator tier).** Estrela runs the model the compact nationalised; quote datelined Mar 2026. Announcement/ceremonial tiers excluded by name. Upgrade path logged.
3. **No savior / no morality tale / no verdict — PASS.** The piece refuses both available clichés: it cuts the triumphal read ("66% is the country; it is not the child"; "whether the floor did") *and* the cynical read ("It would be easy, and wrong, to read that gap as the states marking their own homework"). It credits what is genuinely rare (publishing the audit beside the boast) without crowning Brazil.
4. **Figure discipline — PASS (the reason to exist).** Applies and *extends* ruling #12: prints the self-administered and external numbers side by side, attributes each, names the gap as the finding, and adds the comparability-vs-independence distinction. Honors ruling #4 (named surfaces) and the measured-vs-aspiration distance (66% vs the decree's literal 100%) without collapsing it.
5. **Teaches something durable — PASS.** Two transferable takes: (a) *a number changes what schools do only when someone is made to own it* — the made-national → made-local mechanism (the Selo ties federal recognition to the figure); (b) *who holds the ruler* — when grader and graded are the same hand the figure can still be honest, but only if the external audit is published alongside, and a census by the graded unit can beat an external sample on comparability. Seats two new rails (below).
