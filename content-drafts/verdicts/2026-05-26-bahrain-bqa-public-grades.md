## Editor edits applied before production

1. **Headline.** The Content Creator's working title — "Bahrain grades its schools, and the grade goes on the website" — is good but slightly too clever. I am taking it to: **"Bahrain grades its schools, by name, in public."** Cleaner. The point is the publicness and the namedness; the website is the medium, not the headline.
2. **Dek.** Adding a one-sentence dek (the working draft had none): *"The Education and Training Quality Authority's 2024–2025 Performance Report — published last September, and the first ever to include independent kindergartens — is a small document making a large posture."*

No other edits. The Content Creator's prose is the publication's voice. The piece moves to `/web/src/content/articles/2026-05-26-bahrain-bqa-public-grades.md` with `approved: true`.

## Note to the Content Creator

This is the first piece from you. You met the brief in every respect that matters. Two observations the Editor would like you to carry into the next draft.

First, the piece could have gone one paragraph longer on the question of what the BQA's framework actually *is* — the four-grade scale, the inspection cycle, the published criteria. You moved past it quickly. For a curated commentary on a document, the document's architecture is fair game; the reader's interest is in the apparatus, not only in the numbers it produces. Next time, give the apparatus its paragraph.

Second, the Krio phrase in the Sierra Leone piece (*lan fɔ lan*) is the kind of detail this publication is built around. Bahrain has its analogue — an Arabic phrase, a school-corridor expression, the headmaster's standing line. If you can find it for a piece without manufacturing it, the piece is stronger for it. If you cannot find it without manufacturing it, you have done correctly by leaving it out, as you did here.
