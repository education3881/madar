## 1. Qatar — "Spark, and the hand that holds the match"

**Verdict: holds.**

The five-test rubric:

- **Sourcing reality.** Primary URL is gco.gov.qa — the state's own communications office press file on the launch. Verbatim Al Nuaimi quotes verified against that URL. Secondary primary is thepeninsulaqatar.com on WISE 12 — verbatim Yiannouka quote verified. Both opened today on verdict day. The minister-handoff date (12 November 2024 from Al Nuaimi to Al-Khater) is on the gco.gov.qa institutional page.
- **Specificity.** Numbers in the piece are sourced: 44% → 88% ECE doubling (Al Nuaimi at launch), 4 STEM schools by 2026, 2,000+ students across them, 200+ WISE 12 speakers, 60+ core sessions, 3,000+ participants, 5 thematic tracks, eighteen-month mark = May 2026. None of these is approximated. All have a citation chain.
- **Why-now.** Three converging signals: the eighteen-month operational checkpoint on the launch (May 2026 is now), the WISE 12 summit just behind us (November 2025), the post-handoff implementation year. A reader who asks *why this piece, why now* gets a real answer from the calendar.
- **Dignity.** The piece reads Qatar's "Humanity.io" framing as a coherent posture rather than as rhetoric to be deflated. It names what would falsify the strategy (four STEM schools not opening, ECE numbers not moving, *human-centred* not surviving operational pressure) and treats those as the real test, not gotchas. It is generous to both ministers — neither is set up as a foil.
- **Long-arc.** The closing — "the hand that holds the match in 2026 is not the hand that struck it in 2024" — earns its weight. The piece writes the test of the next four years rather than the verdict on the past eighteen months.

**One sentence I would re-read tomorrow:** "The strategy belongs to the state, not to its first author." This is the piece's argumentative center, and it would survive a hostile read from either Al Nuaimi's office or Al-Khater's — it is generous to both and proprietary to neither. Holds.

**Watch:** the *Humanity.io* / startup-suffix paragraph is the piece's most discursive moment. A senior MoEHE official might find it overplayed. On a slow re-read I think the move earns its space — it is what unlocks the human-as-input / human-as-output question that the rest of the piece needs. Logged as a future re-edit possibility, not a correction.

## 2. Indonesia — "The minister insists: not a new curriculum"

**Verdict: holds.**

- **Sourcing reality.** Primary URL is kemendikdasmen.go.id (siaran-pers 13278) — the Ministry's own press release on the 22 July 2025 webinar. The verbatim Mu'ti quote (*Saya tegaskan bahwa Permendikdasmen Nomor 13 Tahun 2025 yang telah terbit bukanlah tentang Kurikulum Baru*) is on that page. The verbatim Toharudin quote (the *penyempurnaan* eight-dimension list) is on the same page. Secondary primary is jakartaglobe.id with Principal Pujianto's verbatim and the 59,000-school figure. Both opened on verdict day.
- **Specificity.** 59,000 schools, two hours/week in primary and junior high, up to five hours in senior high, phased start at grades 5/7/10, 30-unit computer lab at SDN 20 Sepaku, Google partnership for training one million teachers — all anchored.
- **Why-now.** The regulation issued in July 2025; the 2025/26 school year is the first phased rollout; the reform-fatigue context (K13 → Merdeka → now Permen 13) gives the piece its argumentative spine. A reader asks *why this piece now* and gets *because the first cohort is in classrooms this semester*.
- **Dignity.** The piece avoids any *Indonesia has too many curriculum reforms* framing. It treats *bukanlah tentang Kurikulum Baru* as a serious public-record claim from a minister who has thought about what curriculum-change fatigue does to a system, and reads the regulation against that claim rather than dunking on it. The teacher corps and the parent in the village WhatsApp group both appear as agents in the piece, not as passive recipients of policy.
- **Long-arc.** Closes on the test — whether the disavowal of *reform* survives the implementation year — rather than on a prediction. Earned.

**One sentence I would re-read tomorrow:** "The Ministry has signalled a partnership with Google to train at least one million teachers in coding and AI, but the operational test will be how many of those trained teachers actually occupy the elective slot at the village school, and how many remain as the *math teacher who took the AI module*." This is the piece's structural observation about the gap between a national-rollout press release and the village implementation. Hostile reader: a Kemendikdasmen official. Their honest read: this publication has actually looked at the operational mechanics of a coding-and-AI rollout at the village level, which is more than most of the international coverage has done. Holds.

**Watch:** the *bertanggung jawab* closing-word read is a translation move from the Indonesian to the English-language argumentative frame. A native Indonesian editor would, on a slow read, want to verify that the operational implication I'm drawing from the Minister's word choice is consistent with how Mu'ti has used the word elsewhere. I have not been able to cross-check that today. Logged for the Arabic Editor / a future native-Indonesian read, if and when one becomes available. The piece works without that move; the move is editorial colour rather than load-bearing.

## 3. Uruguay — "Nineteen years of teaching the teachers"

**Verdict: holds.**

- **Sourcing reality.** Primary URL is telesemana.com — the 31 March 2025 dispatch with verbatim Haim and Orsi quotes, the EduIA Lab funder list (World Bank / IDB / IDRC-Canada), the 205,000-students and 10,000+ teachers figures in the *Pensamiento Computacional e IA* programme, the 300+ formative offerings per year. Secondary primaries: uruguayxxi.gub.uy (institutional, on the AI-training scale), ceibal.edu.uy (programme institutional), worldbank.org Ceibal brief (2025 publication). All opened today.
- **Specificity.** 18-year anniversary in 2025 (Plan Ceibal launched 2007), 19th year in 2026, 20-year in 2027, 22,300 educators/students in 2025 Summer School, ~50,000 public-school teacher corps in Uruguay, 1-in-5 teachers having taken a Ceibal AI/computational-thinking module, four presidents and three parties since 2007, EduIA Lab launch 28 March 2025. All anchored.
- **Why-now.** EduIA Lab is two months old at piece publication; the 20-year anniversary is approaching in 2027; the political transition (Lacalle Pou → Orsi, March 2025) is fresh enough to make the *institution that survived government change* observation real, not retrospective.
- **Dignity.** The piece does not paint Uruguay as a development darling or a model country. It treats Plan Ceibal as an institution that has done one thing — teach teachers continuously for eighteen years — and asks what compounds when an institution does that one thing without drama. Generous to Folgar (the previous-administration president) and to Haim (the current one) without either becoming a hero.
- **Long-arc.** The closing — "earned at the slow pace of an institution that has not asked for drama in nineteen years" — is the piece's spine. The 2027 anniversary is named as the next checkpoint. Holds.

**One sentence I would re-read tomorrow:** "The institution survived, in other words, the largest predictor of institutional death in Latin America: a change of government." Strong line. Hostile reader: a senior Uruguayan opposition politician. Their honest read: this is a structural observation about a real Uruguayan institutional achievement, not a partisan claim about any one administration. Holds.

**Watch:** the *one in five Uruguayan public-school teachers has taken a Ceibal-run AI or computational-thinking module* arithmetic is derived (10,000+ teachers in the programme ÷ ~50,000 corps). The derivation is reasonable but is the piece's only inferred figure. Logged: if the next Uruguay piece returns to this, anchor against the public-record corps figure from ANEP (Administración Nacional de Educación Pública) directly rather than the round estimate I used.
