## What this piece is

The Philippines has, by statute, ended the mandate that its youngest grades (K–Grade 3) be taught in the child's mother tongue, reverting to Filipino + English and leaving the first language optional only in qualifying monolingual classes. The spine is **not** "country abandons good policy" or "country fixes failing policy." It is the discipline that **the failing score everyone cites (PISA 347) cannot, by its own design, adjudicate the very policy it is wielded against** — the measured-vs-announced ruling (guidebook #9) applied to a *reversal*. The honest object of the piece is the dispute itself and the place it will actually be settled (the law's three-year review).
