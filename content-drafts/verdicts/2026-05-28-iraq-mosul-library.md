## Five-test pass

1. **Sourcing reality.** Pass. The UNDP press release of 19 February 2022 was opened today; every named-human verbatim quote in the piece is from that release. The UNESCO project page for "Restoring the Mosul University Library" (Project 534IRQ1002) is referenced from the search index — it should be opened directly before the next iteration. The Ulster University training-of-trainers visit (22–25 October 2024) is on the public record via Ulster's own press notice. No fabricated voices. The funding chain (Germany via KfW; Netherlands furniture for the library; Australia furniture for the Court) is traced exactly as the UNDP release states.
2. **Specificity.** Pass. Mosul, 19 February 2022, the library's 1921 founding date, the 8,000–10,000 books-burned estimate, the 1,000-seat capacity, the 40 per cent enrolment increase above pre-ISIL levels, the 31 July 2025 close-out of the UNESCO sub-project. Irreducibly Mosuli.
3. **Why now.** Strong pass. The UNESCO sub-project's July 2025 close-out is the live institutional moment — making the library "fully functional" is the news the press release on the reopening could not yet cover. This is the right time to look at the slower work.
4. **Dignity.** Pass. The Iraqi side is given its voice via Zena Ali Ahmad's "strength and resilience of the city" statement, which the piece deliberately allows to lead. The German contribution is acknowledged, but the piece does not invert into a donor-narrative — the 40 per cent enrolment figure is what does the heaviest lifting, and that figure belongs to the city, not to the donors. The line "That is what a development bank says when a development bank says the right thing" is sharper but reads as fair: a development bank speaking development-bank language is not a failing.
5. **Long arc.** Strong pass. 1921 → 2014-17 → 2022 → 2024-25 → 2025+. The closing question is genuine: a library is not finished when its building reopens, and the open question of whether the catalogue is recognised as the same institution is the right one to leave with the reader.

## Voice notes

- "*That is what a development bank says when a development bank says the right thing.*" — kept. This is the publication learning to say something dry and exact about institutional voice without disrespecting the institution. Watch it in future pieces: this construction should not become a tic.
- "Has risen like a phoenix from the ashes" — appears inside Peter Felten's verbatim quote, not in the piece's own voice. Kept as quoted; the publication would not write the phrase otherwise.
- The piece resists, correctly, the temptation to dwell on the destruction. The library-was-burned section is one paragraph. The library-was-rebuilt-and-the-students-came-back section is several. The proportion is correct.
- House style: kept "Moslawi" in the source quotes only; the piece itself uses "Mosul" / "Mosul University" throughout. Do not introduce variant transliterations in the body voice.
