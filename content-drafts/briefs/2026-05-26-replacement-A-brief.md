## The piece

Bahrain's Education and Training Quality Authority (BQA) — the national body that inspects every school in the Kingdom and publishes the verdict — issued its annual Performance Report for the 2024–2025 academic year in May 2025. The report grades 76 government schools, 23 private schools, 22 independent kindergartens, and 19 vocational training institutes. Twenty-four government schools improved; eleven private schools improved by one grade; forty-five government schools stayed where they were. The first round of formal kindergarten reviews has just concluded.

The piece is a slow, careful reading of **what a small Gulf state does when it decides to grade its own schools publicly, name them, and let parents read the report.** Most Gulf countries publish aggregate education statistics. Bahrain publishes school-by-school review reports with named institutions, identified strengths, and identified weaknesses. That is a posture choice. It is the piece.

This is a Madār "curated commentary" — meaning the spine of the piece is one external document (or a small cluster of related documents) read closely, with our commentary doing the framing. We are not breaking news. We are saying: here is a document worth reading slowly, and here is what it tells us about how a particular state has decided to think about quality.

## The frame — what this piece is *for*

Three things, layered:

1. **A reading of the BQA's posture.** What does it tell us that the BQA names schools, publishes the grade, and puts the report on a public website any parent in Manama can open? Compare implicitly — not by enumeration, not by naming peer countries by name — with the more common practice of publishing only aggregate numbers.
2. **A reading of the 2024–2025 numbers.** The improvement numbers (24 government schools improved, 11 private schools improved by one grade) are not a triumphant figure. They are not a damning figure either. They are a working figure. The piece reads the numbers the way an experienced education reader reads them.
3. **The kindergarten reviews.** This is the new thing in the 2024-2025 round: independent kindergartens were formally reviewed for the first time. That is a small institutional event with downstream consequences (parents who can now read a quality report on a place they were considering for their three-year-old). Give it three or four paragraphs.

## What this piece is NOT

- A ranking. We do not list the highest-rated schools, and we do not name "winners."
- An NGO assessment of Bahrain's school system. We are not the World Bank.
- A comparison piece. No "Bahrain vs. UAE vs. Saudi" tables. The publication's geographic comparativism is editorial, not tabular.
- A diplomatic piece. We do not flatter the BQA; we do not insinuate failure either. We read the document.
