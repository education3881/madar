## Candidate A — The 17 → 90 arc: Saudi kindergarten enrollment as a thirteen-year transformation

**Topic.** The Saudi Vision 2030 commitment to raise kindergarten enrollment from 17 % (2019 baseline, per Ministry of Education) to 90 % (2030 target); current state at 36.7 % (2024), 40 % (2025 target). The operational pace question: 400,000 new KG seats added; 11,547 new teaching jobs in early childhood (the largest government recruitment since the pandemic).

1. **Institution / programme / document.** Saudi Ministry of Education Office of Early Childhood Education; Vision 2030 Human Capability Development Program; Early Learning Standards and 0-6 National Curriculum; Virtual Kindergarten (founded 2019). Documented in UNESCO ECE Saudi Arabia country profile and the Ministry's kindergarten page.
2. **Named humans available for verbatim quotation.** Yousef Al-Benyan, Minister of Education since September 2022, has Arab News verbatim quotes from HCI 2025 (Riyadh, April 13 2025) on the Human Capability frame: *"Our world is evolving faster than ever, driven by technological leaps, environmental sustainability, challenges, and also demographic shifts and global uncertainties."* / *"Yet, amidst these complexities, one important constant remains: human capability, the boundless potential of our people."* / *"We need to define the future roles of teachers — whether they remain a delivery model or transition into facilitators."* On the specific 17 → 90 ECE claim, the quote chain is **reported speech** via the Saudi Press Agency, relayed through Argaam (March 2024): *"Saudi Arabia improved its position in early education from 10 % in 2019 to current 34 %, aiming to reach 90 % by 2030, said Minister of Education Yousef Al-Benyan."* The Argaam paraphrase is sourced to SPA but is not Al-Benyan's verbatim sentence. **Gap:** the piece would benefit from a second named human at the operational tier — an ECE programme lead, a kindergarten director in a specific city, or a teacher-training official — quoted verbatim at a primary URL. Not located in today's recon.
3. **Primary URLs.** All resolved today.
   - https://www.arabnews.com/node/2596855/business-economy (Al-Benyan HCI 2025 verbatim, EN-original)
   - https://www.argaam.com/en/article/articledetail/id/1709225 (SPA-relayed Al-Benyan March 2024 on 17 → 90)
   - https://moe.gov.sa/en/education/generaleducation/pages/kindergarten.aspx (Ministry institutional)
   - https://www.unesco.org/en/early-childhood-education/empowering-early-childhood-education-saudi-arabia-programmes-and-initiatives (UNESCO ECE Saudi profile)
   - https://www.vision2030.gov.sa/en/explore/programs/human-capability-development-program (Vision 2030 institutional)
4. **Why-now signal.** Twelve-month window: HCI 2025 (April), Invest in Education Forum 2025 (February), Madrasati 1447 platform launch (October 2025), 36.7 % 2024 figure published. The 40 % 2025 milestone year is a real inflection.
5. **Dignity check (10 words).** "Reads as institutional architecture, not as a deficit story." Yes.

**Editor's verdict on Candidate A.** Strong topic, strong supporting sources, but the named-quoted-human apparatus is **one human deep, and partly reported speech.** The 2026-05-25 failure mode was thin sourcing on the operational tier; this candidate has the same shape on its operational tier (the kindergarten classroom). **Not ready for Stage 2 in this session.** Carry to 2026-05-31 with a sourcing follow-up commissioned: locate one named ECE administrator or teacher in Riyadh, Jeddah, or a regional centre, quoted verbatim at a primary Arabic-language URL. The Arabic Editor would verify before draft.

## Candidate B — The Invest in Education Forum and what changes when "investment opportunity" enters the K-12 vocabulary

**Topic.** Saudi Ministry of Education's first Invest in Education Forum (Riyadh, 25-26 February 2025) commissioned the Madares platform (school assessment) and the NAFS national-tests programme, and pitched 50 billion riyals of investment opportunities by 2030, targeting 25 % private-sector participation (up from 17 %). A national-state publicly inviting private capital into K-12 at scale.

1. **Institution / programme / document.** Ministry of Education; Madares platform; NAFS national tests; Business Center and three advisory councils.
2. **Named humans available for verbatim quotation.** Al-Benyan, with multiple verbatim quotes from the MoE-controlled news page: *"Our government is making an unprecedented investment in education, with a budget that aligns with the requirements of growth and development. In 2025, the budget designated for education stands as the second largest allocation, totaling 201 billion riyals."* / *"Our objective is for Saudi education to rank among the top 20 educational systems worldwide, in alignment with the goals of Saudi Vision 2030."*
3. **Primary URL.** https://moe.gov.sa/en/mediacenter/moenews/pages/news1_25022025.aspx (resolved today; MoE-controlled primary).
4. **Why-now signal.** Forum held three months ago; signed agreements with the Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs, Infrastructure Fund, Education and Training Evaluation Commission, SME Bank, and Meem Ain Endowment Education Company.
5. **Dignity check (10 words).** Sensitive — risks reading as moralising about marketisation; survivable with care.

**Editor's verdict on Candidate B.** Sources strong, named-quoted human at primary URL solid. **But this would be the third consecutive Madār piece in the "Arabic K-12 platform partners with foreign capital / external institutional layer" envelope** — after Egypt EKB × Elsevier (2026-05-27) and UAE Madrasa × TikTok (2026-05-27). The 2026-05-30 second-read verdict on those two pieces just confirmed both hold, but a third in the same envelope this close together is **repetition, not range.** Issue 02 deserves a different shape. **Pass on editorial grounds. Park for a later cycle (~Q3 2026) when the envelope is no longer dominant on the front page.**
