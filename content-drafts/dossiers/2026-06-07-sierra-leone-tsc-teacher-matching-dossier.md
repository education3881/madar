## TIER 1 — Independently corroborated (safe to write, cite the corroborating source)

Each of these was confirmed today (2026-06-07) by independent web search returning multiple sources, not a single press release.

- **2,341 teachers recruited in the 2024 deployment round.** Corroborated. *Caveat to resolve:* the secondary summaries blur "recruited" with "passed the licensing exam" — one phrasing is "2,341 teachers passed the licensing exam and were recruited." The brief separately claims "100% of selected teachers passed the licensing exam." These are two different statements about the same cohort. The draft must say only what the technical report supports; until then, write "2,341 teachers recruited" and **do not** assert the 100%-pass framing as a separate achievement.
- **Marian Abu — Director of Teacher Management, Teaching Service Commission.** Confirmed, role and title exact. The EdTech Hub conversation *"From algorithm outputs to classroom impact"* is real and datelined **4 December 2024**. She is the anchor voice; this clears Test 2.
- **The partnership structure.** TSC (protagonist) with EdTech Hub, the Learning Generation Initiative (LGI), and Fab Inc. as technical partners. Confirmed across multiple sources. The savior-narrative tripwire holds: the TSC owns the mechanism; the partners are labelled partners.
- **The equity problem the algorithm targets.** Sierra Leone has one of the highest pupil-to-qualified-teacher ratios in West Africa; remote/rural schools are chronically under-staffed; female teachers are sharply underrepresented on payroll in remote communities. Confirmed — a corroborating figure surfaced: **more than 70% of teachers are male**, which is the concrete justification for the female-favouring tiebreaker. Safe to write the tiebreaker *with* this figure.
- **The matching criteria.** District, school level, pupil-to-payroll-teacher ratio, licensed status, school-approval status, distance, remoteness, qualification, and gender. Confirmed as the algorithm's inputs — useful for the "how it actually works" beat the brief demands.
- **TMIS context.** The Teacher Management Information System was operationalised by the TSC in **January 2024**; it has processed licensing exams for thousands of candidates. This both dates the system and supports the "data-integration" limit (the TMIS↔census gap is real because the TMIS is new).

## TIER 3 — DISCREPANCY: resolve before writing either number

- The brief says **57% to disadvantaged districts** (flat).
- An independent source (Teacher Task Force / GPE-adjacent reporting) instead reports **56% of ~2,000 new teacher positions and 53% of ~1,000 early-childhood-development positions** assigned to the **10 of 16 districts** classified as disadvantaged.

These are not the same claim. The "57%" may be a rounded blend, a different denominator, or a different cycle. **Writing "57%" without resolving this risks Test 1.** The draft should either (a) cite the technical report's exact figure once opened, or (b) use the more defensible, sourced construction: *"the majority of new posts — and just over half of early-years posts — went to the ten districts the ministry classifies as disadvantaged."* Decision deferred to the Editor.
