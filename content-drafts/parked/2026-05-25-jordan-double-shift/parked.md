**Parked by:** Editor

**Status:** Parked. Will not run.

## What the source apparatus claimed

The piece, drafted for Issue 01, presented itself as a curated reading of a "2025 progress note from Jordan's Ministry of Education on the schools that run two school days inside one building." The framing was that the ministry had issued an unusually candid accounting of the double-shift system, twelve years on. The piece carried, at publish time, the visible "Draft — pending source verification" pill, in tacit acknowledgement that the central source had not yet been confirmed.

## What verification revealed

On 2026-05-26 the founder reviewed the piece and confirmed what the production-readiness review on 2026-05-25 had already flagged: **the central source did not, on verification, exist in the form the piece claimed.** The "Ministry of Education progress note" was a plausible-sounding placeholder; no document of that description at the cited URL resolved to the artifact the piece described; and the named figures within the piece (enrolment in double-shift schools, percentage of refugee-receiving schools running afternoon shifts, etc.) could not be matched to a primary-source ministerial document.

Madār's editorial rubric, in its restructured form (`/agents/02_editor.md` §"Your verdict rubric", Test 1 — Sourcing reality), treats this as a single-step park. A piece whose central source cannot be opened, today, at the URL the piece names, is not a Madār piece. It is a draft. The pill was the wrong remedy; the right remedy was to hold.

## What would have to change for the topic to be approachable again

The double-shift system in Jordan is a real and editorially interesting topic. The publication is not closing the door on it. It is closing the door on the specific draft that ran with the specific source apparatus that did not stand up.

For the topic to return to the editorial calendar, three conditions must be met:

1. **A specific primary document, opened by the Editor at the time of brief-writing.** A Jordanian Ministry of Education circular, a Queen Rania Foundation report, a UNICEF country-office document with a verifiable PDF URL, a peer-reviewed paper in a journal with a DOI — any one of these, named and opened, would suffice. A vaguely-attributed "progress note" would not.
2. **At least one named human in the country, in role, with their name visible in print elsewhere in 2025–2026.** A double-shift school principal whose name appears in Jordanian press; a Ministry of Education spokesperson whose name appears in a wire-service report; an education researcher at the University of Jordan whose recent paper is the spine of the piece. Anonymous officials and composite teachers, in this topic area, are not allowed. The reader has to be able to trace each claim to a human in the public record.
3. **A "why now" that is more than the calendar.** The double-shift system has existed in Jordan for over a decade. The 2025-vintage version of the topic would need a hook — a new policy, a published evaluation, a measurable change — to earn its present tense.

If all three conditions are met, the Editor will brief the Content Creator on the topic again. Until then, the topic is parked.
