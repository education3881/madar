## 1. The story in one line (the Madār signature)

**"The literacy target, made national."** Brazil took a *municipally*-proven model — Ceará's two-decade *Programa Alfabetização na Idade Certa* (PAIC), born in Sobral — and in 2023 made it a **federal compact** with a single annual number attached. The national figure then moved three years running (56 → 59.2 → 66%). The honest tension is **the spread, not the mean**: Ceará reads 85.3% while the country reads 66%, and the headline indicator is *administered by the states themselves*, not by the federal assessor — so the number that travels (Indicador) and the number that audits (Saeb) **disagree by design**. The seam between those two instruments is the piece.

This is **not a reversal** (the recent corpus is reversal-heavy: Korea, Philippines). It is a *made-measurable / made-national* story, which is the variety the corpus needs alongside the LatAm geography (currently just 1 of 17 pieces — Uruguay).

## 3. The figure (figure gate — measurement side: CLOSED)

**Indicador Criança Alfabetizada (ICA).** Annual % of **2nd-year students in public networks** reaching adequate reading/writing. Built by **INEP**: items elaborated and supplied by INEP, **applied by the state systems**, complementary to Saeb. Test = **16 multiple-choice + 3 open** (incl. a text production); **cutoff 743 points**; ≈ **2 million students** assessed (Oct–Nov window).

| Cycle | National | Notes | Source tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **2023** | **56.0%** | baseline year (decree year) | INEP, published Jun 2024 |
| **2024** | **59.2%** (+3.2pp) | 11 states hit that year's target; 18 improved; Ceará **85.3%** (already above the 2030 target of 80%) | INEP, published Jul 2025 |
| **2025** | **66%** (vs **64% target**) | released **23 Mar 2026**; **19 states + DF (20 units)** met the year's target; announced by Min. Camilo Santana + Pres. Lula at the Selo ceremony | INEP, **`download.inep.gov.br/avaliacao_da_alfabetizacao/resultados/brasil_2025.pdf`** |
| 2025 target | 64% | interim | MEC |
| 2030 target | 80% | the binding horizon | Decreto/MEC |

**LOAD-BEARING FENCE — the two-instruments-disagree rule (figure honesty).** The Indicador is **state-administered**; the federal **Saeb** is **externally administered**. For the same period these disagree materially — **Indicador 56% vs Saeb ~49%** — and MEC has said publicly the federal exam "is not the indicator." They are **different currencies**: a self-administered measure and an external audit measure. **Report both; lead with neither uncritically; the gap is the credibility seam, not a rounding error.** (This generalises ruling #9 *measured-vs-announced* into *self-administered-vs-external* — see the new guidebook note `2026-06-24-self-administered-vs-external-assessment.md`.)

## 6. Counter-texture (mandatory — what an honest piece must carry)

1. **The disaggregation gap.** No public breakdown by race/colour or socioeconomic level → cannot say whether gains are *equitable* or are concentrated. State it as a limitation, in print (Todos Pela Educação technical note, Mar 2026).
2. **Comparability is still maturing.** INEP *Instrução Normativa nº 02/2025* exists precisely to strengthen comparability of the 2nd-year measurement across states — i.e. the instrument itself concedes it is being hardened. A rising number partly reflects a settling instrument.
3. **The self-administered seam** (§3 fence) — the headline is administered by the same systems it grades.
4. **"100% by end of 2nd year"** (the decree's literal goal) vs the measured 66% — name the distance honestly; this is an aspiration instrument, not a forecast.
