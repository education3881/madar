# RECON BRIEF — Vietnam's move to tuition-free public-school education

**Date of recon:** 2026-06-14
**Researcher:** Madār Researcher
**Status:** Tier-1 source reconnaissance for park-or-proceed decision. NOT a draft.

---

## CANDIDATE SUMMARY

In 2025 Vietnam moved, in two stages, to make public-school education tuition-free from the 2025–2026 school year for all students from preschool through upper-secondary (grade 12): first a **Politburo decision of 28 February 2025**, then a binding **National Assembly resolution (Resolution 217/2025/QH15) passed 26 June 2025** (440 of 441 deputies in favour). The story's real angle is NOT "Vietnam made school free" — it is the gap between **tuition ("học phí")** being waived and **education being free**. Tuition in Vietnam is a relatively small, capped monthly fee; the larger household costs — compulsory health insurance, semi-boarding/meal services, uniforms, textbooks, extra/after-school classes, and the recurring "parents' fund" — remain. The policy is real, well-sourced, and quantified; the angle is a clean, defensible "tuition-free ≠ fee-free" explainer with named official voices and concrete fee figures.

## POLICY INSTRUMENT (verified)

Two distinct instruments — both must be named to be accurate:

1. **Politburo decision — 28 February 2025.** The Communist Party Politburo decided to waive all tuition fees for public-school students from preschool to the end of upper-secondary (THPT) nationwide, from the 2025–2026 school year (September 2025). It tasked the Government / Ministry of Education and Training (MOET) / Ministry of Finance to build the implementation plan.
   - Vietnam Government Portal (VGP / Báo Chính phủ, EN), 28 Feb 2025 — https://en.baochinhphu.vn/viet-nam-to-exempt-tuition-for-all-public-school-pupils-from-2025-2026-academic-year-111250228202316262.htm
   - Government Portal (VN, policy desk): https://xaydungchinhsach.chinhphu.vn/bo-chinh-tri-quyet-dinh-mien-hoc-phi-cho-hoc-sinh-pho-thong-cong-lap-11925022817591807.htm

2. **National Assembly Resolution No. 217/2025/QH15 — passed 26 June 2025.** The 15th National Assembly passed the resolution on tuition exemption and support for preschool children and general-education students across the national education system, approved 440/441 deputies. It takes effect from the date of adoption and applies from the 2025–2026 academic year. (A companion Resolution 218/2025/QH15 universalises preschool education for ages 3–5.) Note: VietnamPlus reports adoption 26 June; some outlets cite the NA Chairman Trần Thanh Mẫn signing on 3 July 2025 — adoption vs signing dates; flag both, do not conflate.
   - VietnamPlus (Vietnam News Agency, EN), 26 Jun 2025 — https://en.vietnamplus.vn/na-resolutions-on-tuition-and-agricultural-land-tax-exemption-universal-preschool-education-passed-post321705.vnp

**Intermediate step (useful context):** On ~17 Apr 2025 MOET, via Minister **Nguyễn Kim Sơn**, proposed *extending* the exemption beyond public schools — to also *support* (subsidise) tuition for preschool-to-grade-12 students in private/non-public schools at the public-tuition rate. This is why the final resolution covers both: **full exemption for public-institution students** and **partial tuition support for private/non-public students** (capped at the public rate; families pay the difference). — vietnamnet.vn / vietnam.vn coverage of MOET proposal.

## SCOPE (verified)

- **Who is covered:** Preschool / kindergarten (mầm non), primary (tiểu học), lower-secondary (THCS), and upper-secondary (THPT) — i.e. the full preschool-through-grade-12 span. Roughly **23 million students** nationwide.
- **Public vs private:** Public-institution students = **full tuition exemption**. Private / non-public (dân lập, tư thục) students = **state tuition *support*** at a level set by the Provincial People's Council, not exceeding the public-school tuition rate; the private/public differential is still paid by families.
- **Already-free baseline (important nuance for accuracy):** Primary-school tuition was *already* free under prior law; 5-year-old preschoolers were exempted from 1 Sep 2024 under Decree 81. So the 2025 policy's genuinely *new* coverage is principally **lower- and upper-secondary** plus non-5-year-old preschool, plus the private-school *support* track. (Source: Tuoi Tre, 11 Mar 2025; baochinhphu.)
- **Effective date:** From the **2025–2026 school year (September 2025)**, nationwide, not phased.

## THE TUITION-FREE ≠ FEE-FREE DISTINCTION (the angle)

This is the heart of the story and it is **well sourced to primary Vietnamese outlets**, not just Western commentary.

**What "tuition" (học phí) legally is:** a capped monthly fee set by each Provincial/Municipal People's Council within central limits. It is small. Per the Government Portal: preschool tuition runs **VND 50,000–540,000/month**; upper-secondary **VND 50,000–650,000/month**, varying by urban/rural and region (baochinhphu, 28 Feb 2025). RFA cites HCMC figures of ~VND 100,000–200,000/month (high school) and ~VND 300,000 (preschool). Converting at ~VND 25,500/USD: roughly **US$2–25/month**.

**What is NOT waived (still paid by families), per Tuoi Tre's explainer "Được miễn học phí, học sinh phải đóng những khoản nào?" (Tuoi Tre, reporter Mỹ Dung, 11 Mar 2025):**
- **Compulsory health insurance (bảo hiểm y tế):** students must enrol; the state subsidises 30–100% of the premium by priority group, but it is a required out-of-pocket line for most.
- **Education-service / support fees (khoản thu dịch vụ, hỗ trợ hoạt động giáo dục):** set per school under the Provincial People's Council resolution, charged on individual demand — e.g. semi-boarding (bán trú) supervision & hygiene, breakfast service, after-hours care, initial health checks, caregiver-staff service, school transport. Tuoi Tre gives HCMC 2024–25 figures: caregiver-staff service up to **VND 260,000/student/month** (nursery groups 1–2) and **VND 160,000/month** (kindergarten groups 1–2).
- **The "parents' fund" (quỹ phụ huynh / quỹ trường, quỹ lớp):** recurring contributions for Teachers' Day, Women's Day, extracurriculars — legally must be voluntary (Circular 55/2011/TT-BGDĐT bars the parents' committee from compelling non-voluntary contributions), but widely reported as a de facto burden.
- **Textbooks, uniforms, supplies, transport, extra/after-school classes (học thêm).** RFA cites a Hanoi parent paying VND 1–2 million/month on tuition+meals+water and a separate VND 325,000 after-school fee.

**The framing line for the Editor:** Vietnam waived a fee that was already among the smallest line-items in a family's education budget; the costs that actually deter low-income families — meals, the parents' fund, extra classes, uniforms, books — are untouched. Vietnamese state and social media have flagged "over-collection" (lạm thu) at public schools for years (Tuoi Tre; RFA). This is a globally recurrent pattern ("free education often isn't free" — RFA's Southeast Asia survey covering Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, Myanmar).

**Reference earnings anchor:** average monthly worker income in Vietnam in 2024 ≈ **VND 7.7 million (~US$300)** (General Statistics Office, via RFA) — useful for sizing the residual-cost burden.

## FISCAL LINE

- **Headline annual cost:** **~VND 30,600 billion (≈ VND 30.6 trillion) per year** from the state budget to exempt/support tuition preschool-to-grade-12 nationwide. RFA rounds to "~VND 30 trillion / US$1.17 billion"; other outlets give US$1.2 billion. (At ~VND 25,500/USD, VND 30.6tn ≈ **US$1.2 billion**.)
- **First-year sub-figure:** estimated **~VND 22,500 billion** to exempt tuition for 5-year-old preschoolers + primary + lower-secondary in 2025–2026 (vietnam.vn / MOET briefing) — appears to be a partial-scope figure, not the full annual total; treat the two figures as different scopes, not contradictory.
- **A third figure in circulation:** a MOET *proposal* phase cited "~VND 31.4 trillion" (vietnam.vn). Likely the pre-final estimate. Do not present as the enacted number.
- **Who pays:** State budget. Tuition rates are set by **Provincial/Municipal People's Councils** within central caps, and the budget burden is shared — central guarantees the framework; provinces/municipalities (e.g., Hanoi, HCMC) already fund local exemptions and meal-support top-ups (Hanoi: VND 3,000 billion for primary lunch support, July 2025, per Tuoi Tre event line). The exact central-vs-provincial split is **not nailed in a single primary source I verified** — see Figure Gate.
