# Designer's mood-board brief — 3 visual directions for Madār

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**By:** Designer agent
**For decision by:** Vini

## The unifying gesture

The macron over the *ā* in **Madār** — and its mirror, the *alif madda* over the alif in **مدار** — is the brand's smallest, most repeatable unit. A rule. A horizon. A line of latitude. Whichever direction is chosen, that horizontal bar is what people will remember. All three directions treat it as the only ornament the wordmark needs.

---

## Direction 01 — Cairo Print Room

A publication that looks like it was printed on a state press in 1968 and recovered: considered, slightly austere, the voice of a regional intellectual quarterly that takes its readers seriously without performing it.

**References:** *Al-Majalla* and *Hiwar* magazines (Beirut/Cairo, 1960s); Helmi El-Touni's book covers for Dar el-Fata el-Arabi (1970s); Karel Martens' *Printed Matter*; Migrant Journal's editorial discipline.

**Palette:**
- `#F2EBDC` — uncoated newsprint cream (paper)
- `#1A1A1A` — soft black (ink)
- `#9B2C2C` — oxidized Cairo red (accent, sparing)
- `#2C4A3E` — bottle green (secondary accent, contents/Arabic-script issues)
- `#C9B896` — muted ochre (tonal fields, never type)

**Typography:**
- Latin: commissioned wordmark in spirit of **Plantin / Lyon Display**; body **Tiempos** or **Lyon Text**. Heavy slab macron, not hairline.
- Arabic: **29LT Zarid Display**; body **29LT Zarid Text** or **TPTQ Naskh**.
- Same x-height and color on both scripts.

**Hero treatment:** Document photography and archival material, full-bleed. Real things, photographed straight. No illustration, no AI. When photography isn't available, the hero is a single 240pt pull-quote in Zarid Display, with the macron separating Arabic and Latin stacks.

**Signals:** This publication has a memory and a politics.
**Refuses:** Nostalgia, pan-Arab kitsch, tribute act to mid-century Beirut.
**Risk:** Tips into "heritage" solemnity. Mitigated by rigorous photo editing and not letting the red become decorative.

---

## Direction 02 — Field Atlas

A publication that behaves like a research instrument — a working atlas of education in places the global press doesn't map. Drier, more diagrammatic, closer to a journal than a magazine. The artsy vibe comes from the *data* being treated as image.

**References:** Phaidon's *Atlas of Brutalist Architecture*; Migrant Journal; *MAP Magazine*; Tufte (warmed up); Irma Boom's *SHV Think Book*.

**Palette:**
- `#FAFAF7` — bright warm white (paper, slightly off — this matters)
- `#0E1B2C` — deep navy (primary ink)
- `#D94F2A` — survey-marker orange (data accent only, never type)
- `#7A8471` — sage / topo-map green (secondary data accent)
- `#E8E2D0` — sand tone for ground fills on maps

**Typography:**
- Latin: commissioned wordmark from **GT Sectra / Domaine Display** family. Macron rendered as a cartographic scale bar — fine line with perpendicular tick marks. Body: **Söhne** or **ABC Diatype Mono** for captions, **GT Sectra Text** for long-form.
- Arabic: **Greta Arabic** (display) — rationalist pairs with Sectra. **Adelle Arabic** (body) for legibility at small sizes.

**Hero treatment:** Commissioned information graphics — custom maps, diagrams, isotypes, data drawings. Made for the page, hand-drafted, credited like photography. When qualitative, the hero is a single labelled object photograph (textbook, uniform, chalkboard) shot like a museum specimen.

**Signals:** This publication treats the places it covers as worth measuring with precision, not just describing with feeling.
**Refuses:** Infographic-spam, corporate data viz, McKinsey aesthetics.
**Risk:** Reads as cold if the writing doesn't carry the warmth the design withholds. Editorial voice must do that work.

## Direction 03 — Wide Margin

A literary publication first, design second — type is the picture; the few images are chosen with the weight of a single sentence in a poem.

**References:** *The Paris Review*, *Granta*; Apartamento (stripped of Spanish whimsy); Hassan Massoudy's calligraphic compositions; Mevis & Van Deursen's Stedelijk work.

**Palette:**
- `#EFEAE0` — warm bone (paper)
- `#241F1A` — espresso-brown ink, never pure black (this is the signature move)
- `#B85C38` — terracotta (used once per issue, on the cover macron only)
- `#5B6B5A` — slate-olive (folios, captions, marginalia)
- **No fourth color.** The restraint is the identity.

**Typography:**
- Latin: fully commissioned wordmark cut by someone like **Kris Sowersby** or **Radim Peško** — contemporary transitional serif with one eccentric detail. Macron drawn as calligraphic reed-pen stroke, thicker left, tapering right. Body: **Freight Text Pro**.
- Arabic: custom or semi-custom Naskh from **TPTQ Arabic** or **29LT**. The *alif madda* mirrors the Latin macron stroke. Body: **TPTQ Naskh**.

**Hero treatment:** Commissioned illustration — slow, single-image, often black-and-one-color, from a rotating roster of illustrators from the regions covered. Some pieces have NO image — the hero is the headline, set huge, on its own page, with a 50%-empty facing page. The empty page is not a mistake; it is the design.

**Signals:** Reading this is going to take an hour, and that hour is the point.
**Refuses:** Scandinavian-startup minimalism (this is *generous* whitespace, a different posture).
**Risk:** Demands extraordinary writing. Mediocre prose collapses this direction immediately — the design has nowhere to hide.

---

## Designer's honest preference

**Direction 02 — Field Atlas.** The publication's stance is geo-political; the founder said so explicitly. Direction 01 carries that through aesthetic memory; Direction 03 through literary weight; Direction 02 through **method** — and method is harder to fake, harder to copy, and ages better. A magazine that maps Sierra Leone's ECE provision with the same diagrammatic seriousness that *Migrant Journal* maps borders is making a political argument on every spread without raising its voice. It also offers the most defensible long-term commissioning model: cartographers and diagram-makers from the regions covered, credited like photographers, building a roster nobody else has.

- Direction 01: safest and prettiest
- Direction 03: most beautiful and most fragile
- Direction 02: turns Madār into an *instrument* rather than a *publication*

If 02 is picked, first commission is the wordmark; second commission, same week, is an illustrator-cartographer to set the diagrammatic house style across the first three issues.
