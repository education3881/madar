## What's real vs. what's placeholder

**Real and ready for production:**

## What I'd ask you to confirm before any commission goes out

1. **The hero taxonomy is the right one.** Cartography, object documentation, isotype, no-image essay. If you want a fifth category (illustration in the literary-magazine sense, or single-piece lettering commissions), say so now — it changes the commissioning roster.
2. **The orange-discipline rule.** Orange = data only, never decoration, never on body type. This is the strictest rule in the system and the easiest one for a future contributor to break without realising. Confirm you want it held.
3. **The bilingual logic.** Every Arabic-geography piece carries a parallel Arabic paragraph — not a translation. That doubles the Editor's writing load on Gulf / Levant / North Africa pieces. Confirm the throughput trade-off is worth it.

## The first commission I'd brief out this week

The single typographic event the mark organises around is the macron over the *ā* in Madār and its mirror in the *alif madda* of مدار. The mark must hold at 8mm (spine) and 400mm (cover); it carries no icon and no locked tagline. Cost should land in the £2k–£4k range from a serious type designer; I have two candidates in mind from the lists of independents working in cross-script editorial.

Everything else — the cartographer, the object-documentarian, the production typefaces — can wait until the wordmark exists, because the wordmark sets the optical weight every other commission has to match.

## Fifth addendum · 2026-05-25 (later still)

Two corrections from you after seeing the home page rendered, both applied.

**1. The wordmark is now real type.** You were right that the v0.2 path-outlined mark looked crude — hand-drafting legible Latin and Arabic glyph outlines is what professional type designers spend years learning, and my approximation was reading exactly as that: an approximation. The fix was Approach A from your brief: I extracted glyph outlines **directly from the open-source font files** — Newsreader (Latin, SIL OFL) and Amiri (Arabic Naskh, SIL OFL) — using `fontTools`. The wordmark is now the actual Newsreader glyphs for `M a d a r` and the actual Amiri glyphs for `م د ا ر`, with the Arabic correctly shaped (mim takes its initial form, dal its final form, alif and ra their isolated forms — typographically correct for the word مدار).
