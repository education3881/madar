# The Still — production notes

> **Note · 2026-05-25.** The convention previously documented in this file (the *Signature Mark*, an authored rule-based geometric composition with a captioned rule) was retired and replaced with the **Still** — a painterly, atmospheric, in-house SVG composition. With v0.3 (later same day) the Still convention has now been refined again: it is no longer mood-only abstraction. The Still is now **soft colour volumes (atmosphere) + a continuous-line drawing of a concrete element from the article's subject (subject)**. The line is the figure; the volumes are the ground. The spec lives at `SYSTEM.md` §4b; the rationale for both changes is in `_NOTE_FOR_VINI.md`. This document is kept under the old filename so existing links resolve; a future v0.4 will move it to `_STILL.md`.

**Convention name:** the Still · **سكون** (*sukūn*).
**Spec home:** `SYSTEM.md` §4b.
**Frequency:** one per article. No exceptions.
**Format:** hand-drafted inline SVG with two stacked registers — (1) soft colour-volume atmosphere from gradients, washes, blur, grain; (2) a continuous-line drawing in ink of a concrete subject from the article. The line carries painterly stroke-width variation; the atmosphere uses the five-token palette softly.
**Caption on the page:** none. The Still carries no caption text. A short colophon line under the composition names the article number and the Still maker, and that is all. Mood-words *and* line-subject are logged here, not on the page.
**Sourcing:** produced in-house by the design lead in SVG. Public-domain archival artwork may be colour-graded into the palette and used as a Still in occasional cases (see *Adjacent paths* below). No commissions in v0.1. Zero budget.

The Still is the publication's *illustrated* gesture per piece — the place where the article gets a picture made for it. The hero is the *instrument* (cartography, object, isotype, empty page); the Still is the *illustration*. It coexists with the hero; it does not replace it. The Still is what makes the publication feel *authored* rather than *aggregated*: every article carries its own drawn scene, drawn for it, in the same hand.

The phone-wallpaper test is the operative standard. If a reader would not save the composition to their phone as a lock-screen image, it is not a Still and does not run.

### Field Note 01 · *The teacher in Bo who still uses last term's chalk* · Sierra Leone

**Atmosphere mood-words:** *dawn light · the room before the room is full · the chalk that was left from last term · patience · the first heat.*

**Line subject:** a teacher's chalkboard on an A-frame easel, with last term's arithmetic still half-erased on it, a stick of chalk on the lower rail, a teacher's hand suggested at the right edge of the board.

The atmosphere is a soft dawn-warm radial wash breathing in from the upper right, with a sage-mud floor pooling along the lower band — the warm, dim early-morning light of a classroom before the children arrive. The line draws the board in confident, weight-varied ink strokes: easel legs in heavier downstrokes, a lighter crossbar, the slate face painted as a dark interior the chalk-strokes sit inside. The chalk writing is itself drawn — a column of two-digit numbers ("12 / 8 / +37 / __ / 55"), a date in cursive, a half-erased sweep across the middle of the board, four tally marks at the bottom-right of the board with their diagonal crossbar. A small stick of chalk rests on the lower rail. A few specks of orange chalk-dust drift in the dawn air at the right.

The composition is the experience of arriving early in a classroom that has not been cleaned since last term — a teacher about to begin again, with the materials that are still here.

**Where it sits:** between the body subhead *What the chiefdom map shows* and *A note on the ratio*, full-width of the article container.

**Made by:** Design Lead, in-house, in inline SVG.

### Field Note 02 · *The civics page that changed three times* · Jordan

**Atmosphere mood-words:** *erasure-and-return · soft strata · the ghost of an earlier reading · quiet contemplation.*

**Line subject:** an open textbook lying flat. The same single page is drawn in three slightly offset positions — the page rewritten three times. One sentence on the right page is in orange — the line that changed. Three small orange ticks in the right margin mark the editions.

The atmosphere is the layered-strata palimpsest the article needs: a deep navy-sage stratum pools below, a sand wash sits middle, a warm wash drifts upper. Soft bloom-marks where ink once sat. The book sits inside this palimpsest, not over it — the strata read as the *history of the page*, the book as the *page as it sits now*. The current (2023) textbook is drawn in heaviest line, with a small lifted corner that suggests the page is being turned. Behind it, two fainter ghost outlines mark the 2014 and 2018 positions of the same page — the page that has occupied this position three times. A single orange line of type on the right page marks the sentence that changed. Three small orange tick-marks in the right margin name the editions.

The composition is the experience of reading the page that has been re-written and reading the older versions through the newer one.

**Where it sits:** between the body subhead *The 2023 sentence* and *Three pages 42, side by side*, full-width of the article container.

**Made by:** Design Lead, in-house, in inline SVG.

### Curated 01 · *Escuela Nueva at fifty* · Colombia

**Atmosphere mood-words:** *patience · the slow building · hands of many · the village under the mountain · fifty years of practice.*

**Line subject:** a small rural one-room schoolhouse beneath the Andean foothills — pitched roof, bell-cupola with a small cross, two windows, a single door, a flagpole carrying a small orange flag, a chimney. Behind the schoolhouse, mountain peaks drawn as continuous angular lines. A child walks the dashed footpath toward the door. Two small trees flank the building. A ghost-line of an earlier facade sits underneath — "fifty years of practice."

The atmosphere is the publication's interlocking warm-earth colour palette — terracotta, ochre, sage, dusty rose — pools overlapping without absorbing each other, a low band of warm light across the foothill horizon, a deep earth band beneath. The line touches each of those colour pools as it crosses the field: the mountains cross the sage and the ochre, the schoolhouse sits at the foot of the terracotta, the flagpole crosses the rose. The drawing is small at the centre of a wide field; the *village under the mountain* reads as scale.

**Where it sits:** between the body subhead *On the role of the teacher* and the Spanish-language parallel section, full-width of the article container.

**Made by:** Design Lead, in-house, in inline SVG.

## In-house production workflow — how the Still gets made on zero budget

This is the workflow the design lead follows for every piece. It is the operational answer to *what replaces the commission*. It is meant to be repeatable.

**1. Write the atmosphere mood-words AND name the line-subject.** Three to five mood-words for the atmosphere (in plain language: *dawn light, long walking, patience, the first heat*). And one concrete subject the line will draw — the most representational element the article is about (*a chalkboard, an open textbook, a small schoolhouse*). The Editor may contribute mood-words; the Designer holds the final list and the line-subject decision. Both are logged in this file under the article's entry.

**2. Decide the composition's centre and the figure-ground relationship.** The Still has a centre of attention — almost always the line-drawn subject. The atmosphere is the ground; the line is the figure. Where will the subject sit in the field? How will the colour pools relate to it (background, behind, beneath, around)? Sketch on paper or a tablet in 60 seconds. The sketch is not delivered.

**3. Build the palette zones.** Each Still uses 3–4 of the five palette tokens in atmospheric mode. Decide which token will *cover most of the field* (the ground), which two will be *zones* (volumes that have edges that breathe into the ground), and which one — if any — will be the *small accent* (a single warm point, never more). Orange, when present, is the small accent. The line itself is always `--ink`.

**4. Write the SVG `<defs>` block.** At minimum: two linear gradients, one radial gradient, one Gaussian-blur filter, one fractal-noise filter for grain. These are the painting tools for the atmosphere. The rest of the composition is geometry that calls into these defs.

**5. Draw the atmosphere volumes.** Soft-edged rectangles, ellipses, or `<path>` blobs filled with the gradients and blurred. Each volume is a colour zone, not a shape — the blur dissolves the edge into the ground. Keep them gentler than in v0.2 so the line has room to read.

**6. Draw the line-subject — this is the figure.** A continuous-line drawing of the concrete subject, executed as one or more weighted `<path>` strokes. Painterly stroke-width variation: heavier on the downstrokes (walls, stems, vertical members), lighter on the cross-strokes (lintels, lids, horizontals). The line may break, loop, or wobble slightly so the hand reads as hand. Stroke `--ink` at 0.85–0.95 opacity for the primary contour; lower opacity for ghost-echoes and secondary lines. If the article carries a single argument-event (a sentence that changed, a tally that crossed a threshold, a flag), this is the one place a small orange accent on the line is permitted.

**7. Add grain.** A full-field rectangle filled with a fractal-noise filter at 6–10% opacity. The grain breaks the digital flatness that gradients arrive with and matches the painted register the publication is reaching for.

**8. Test against the phone-wallpaper standard.** Open the SVG on a phone. Set it as a lock-screen background. Look at it for five seconds without the article around it. Would you keep it? If no, redraw.

**9. Log the file.** Save the SVG to `/design-assets/stills/article-NN-slug.svg` for archival. The inline copy lives in the article HTML; the standalone copy lives in the archive. The mood-words are added to this gallery file.

Typical time-cost per Still, after the first three are made: **3–5 hours of focused work**. The first three take longer because the visual vocabulary is being established; later Stills compound off the patterns that worked in earlier ones.

## Adjacent paths — occasional, never default

These two paths exist for cases where the in-house SVG is not the best instrument. They are not the workflow; they are the exceptions.

### Public-domain archival artwork, colour-graded

Where a piece's geography has a strong archival record — Maghreb miniatures, West African textile catalogues, Levant lithographs from early-20th-century Beirut presses, South Asian botanical drawings in the Wellcome Collection — a single public-domain artefact may be brought in, colour-graded into the Madār palette, and used as the Still.

**Acceptable sources:**
- **Wellcome Collection** — CC0 and CC-BY items, the latter credited.
- **Rijksmuseum Open Access** — public domain.
- **The Met Open Access** — CC0.
- **Smithsonian Open Access** — CC0.
- **Internet Archive** — license per item, read each one.
- **Library of Congress Free to Use and Reuse** — public domain.
- **Regional library digitization projects** — license per project; not used until license is read.

**Constraint:** the artefact must itself be a *gesture* — a painting, a textile pattern, a calligraphic exercise, a lithographic study — not a documentary photograph. The colour-grade pulls it into the palette: duotone `--ink`/`--paper` with a `--sand` mid-warm shift, or a `--sage` overlay at 8–12%, depending on the source. Logged license, source URL, and accession number in the article HTML's commented credit block.

**Frequency:** at most one in five pieces. Beyond that, the publication starts to feel like a catalogue of found objects, which is a different publication.

### Free-source photography (separate from the Still)

Free-source photography — Unsplash, Pexels, museum open-access photography — is permitted in Madār **only as secondary breathing room mid-article in long pieces**, under §4c of the system spec. It is **never** the Still and **never** the hero. The full rule lives in `SYSTEM.md` §4c.

## Why this convention generalises

The Still convention holds across the publication's geographic and editorial range. The atmosphere mood-words remain easy to write per piece; the line-subject is now the most concrete object or scene the article is about. Five illustrative cases:

- **Iraq · the missing year of schooling in Mosul, 2014–2017.** *Atmosphere*: gap · dust · the smell of an empty schoolyard · a low sand wash with a sage-grey absence breathing across it. *Line*: **a row of empty school desks, drawn in one continuous line — the front desk in heaviest stroke, the desks behind it fading into faint ghost-outlines, the last desk barely a suggestion.** A book left open on the front desk, a child's school bag hanging from the side. The room that wasn't used for three years.
- **Senegal · bilingual Wolof-French primary instruction.** *Atmosphere*: two rivers · the same ground · a meeting that takes its time · two soft volumes of differently-warm colour meeting in a soft fold near the centre. *Line*: **a child's exercise book lying open with two columns of writing — Wolof on the left page, French on the right — drawn in continuous looping strokes that meet at the spine.** A small pencil rests across the gutter.
- **Morocco · the textbook map that no longer marks the Western Sahara border.** *Atmosphere*: the edge that the book does not draw · a wash that fades cleanly across most of the field but stops short of the right edge. *Line*: **an open textbook turned to a page carrying a hand-drawn map of Morocco, the dotted line of the disputed border drawn for half its length and then trailing off into the page-margin.** A pencil resting beside the page, mid-stroke. The map that does not finish itself.
- **Tanzania · school fees abolished 2001, uniforms abolished 2024.** *Atmosphere*: the same long road · twenty-three years · arriving in the same place by a different path · two soft warm bands crossing the field at slightly different angles, meeting once. *Line*: **a child's school uniform on a wire hanger — shirt, shorts, a tie — drawn in one continuous line.** Behind it, a fainter ghost-outline of an older uniform marks 2001; the present uniform is drawn in heaviest stroke. A small orange tag at the collar marks the policy event.
- **Egypt · the Arabic word for literacy in policy, 1953–2023.** *Atmosphere*: a word read four times · the ghost of the older reading · four faint translucent layers descending into the page-tone. *Line*: **a hand holding a piece of chalk, mid-stroke, writing the Arabic word for literacy (محو الأمية) — the word drawn four times, each version slightly different, stacked in soft progression.** The most recent reading is the heaviest line; the three earlier readings sit behind it as fainter ghost-outlines. The progression of policy as the progression of handwriting.

Each is a different scene; none is a different *style*. The publication's coherence is held by the palette discipline and the in-house hand, not by a repeated visual gesture. What unites every Still is the structural pair — soft volumes as ground, continuous-line illustration as figure — drawn by the same hand against the same five tokens.

## What this document used to say, and why it was wrong

Until 2026-05-25, this file documented the **Signature Mark** convention: a rule-based geometric or typographic composition, captioned with the rule that generated it, intended to encode the article's argument into non-documentary visual form. The first issue's two Stills were drafted under that convention — Article 01 as a tally-grid of twenty strokes, Article 02 as three identical squares with a redacted glyph-form.

That convention was retired because, on inspection, it had produced *diagrams of arguments* rather than *images*. The compositions were intellectually defensible — each one's rule could be stated, the rotation principle generalised, the discipline was internally coherent — but they failed the standard the publication actually needs from its atmospheric layer: they were not beautiful on their own terms before the caption explained them. A reader would not have saved one to their phone.

The Still convention replaces the Signature Mark wholesale. The new register is painterly, atmospheric, mood-derived, soft. The new production path is in-house in SVG, against logged mood-words, at zero budget. The old roster of commissioned artists (Diala Brisly, the West African printmaker, the South Asian concrete-poetry artist) is retired — no Stills are commissioned in v0.1.

The change is not a refinement of the previous convention. It is a different conception of what the publication's authored layer is for. The previous one tried to *argue*. The new one tries to *breathe*.

— Designer
