# Growth · launch packet — "The textbook that stopped being one"

**Date:** 2026-06-17 · **Status:** piece goes LIVE (EN + AR) on this push.
**Posture (standing):** lead with return rate, not raw counts; no third-party trackers; engage the list only after they engage first; no subscribe-to-subscribe.

## 1. Why this piece travels

It is the most *regulator-facing* piece in the catalogue. Every education ministry on earth is currently deciding how hard to push AI into classrooms; Korea is the first to have mandated it by statute and then reversed by statute — a natural-experiment other systems will cite. The hook is not "AI failed" (that framing is everywhere and wrong); it is the **"two clocks"** line — *making a tool compulsory is fast; earning a classroom's trust in it is slow* — which is the rare take a policy reader hasn't already seen ten times. That contrarian-but-sourced angle is the share trigger and the return-rate lever: a reader who came for the verdict stays for the frame.

## 2. The cluster extends — the AI-governance rail goes live as a triptych

The related-reading rail now seats Korea in the AI-in-education governance cluster the Editor has been building: **Uruguay (lab-first, opt-in) ↔ Indonesia (elective-first) ↔ Korea (mandate-first, reversed)**. A reader on any of the three has the other two one click away — three different national answers to the same question, which is exactly the "continue reading" a ministry analyst wants. (Reciprocal back-links Uruguay/Indonesia → Korea are deferred to the next daily; the rail renders one-directional and graceful meanwhile.)
