## 3. Durable rulings — the generalizable lessons (apply to every candidate)

These are the transferable rules the source-specific notes paid for. The Researcher applies them at recon; the Editor checks them at verdict.

1. **The operational-not-ceremonial-voice rule (Sierra Leone / Saudi).** When a strategy PDF is the candidate, the unpark hinges on the person who *runs the mechanism*, datelined 2024+ — not the one who *announced* it. Sierra Leone cleared (Marian Abu, on classroom impact, Dec 2024); Saudi and Korea parked (only the launch/announcement tier was reachable). Run this test *before commissioning*.

2. **The tiered-sourcing-asymmetry rule (Saudi).** A government flagship over-documents the minister and the target, under-documents the classroom — in *both* languages. Plan the sourcing around that asymmetry from day one. When the operational tier is unreachable, consider the **data-led inversion** (let an official statistics report carry the why-now, require one named operational voice for dignity) before abandoning the candidate.

3. **The evaluator-on-record trace tier (2026-06-10 ruling — the load-bearing new rule).** A figure may run *without the primary report in hand* only when ALL five hold: (1) the evaluator is on the record by name + affiliation describing the finding; (2) the operator confirms separately by name; (3) the trial design is stated falsifiably (sample/arms/period); (4) every figure carries in-text attribution to the named evaluation, never Madār's own voice; (5) a standing follow-up is logged to re-verify when the report publishes. If any fails: trace to primary or park. A named evaluator describing her own randomised trial *is* a primary source — a person, not a PDF. Verdict checklist line: *Trace tier: ☐ primary in hand · ☐ evaluator-on-record (5 verified, follow-up logged) · ☐ neither → park.*

4. **A program name is not a citation — pin every figure to its report, cycle, and scope.** Two failure modes, same fix (state the discriminator in print next to the number): the **two-studies-same-name trap** (IQRA ~2020 vs 2024–25 J-PAL trial — check year + sample) and the **same-figure-different-scope trap** (Korea ₩533.3B = 2024 project allocation vs ₩1T = teacher training 2024–26; never average or reconcile — fence each to its scope or drop both).

5. **The blocked-tool-is-not-the-unreachable-resource rule (2026-06-08).** A failed `curl` is a statement about `bash`, not about the open web. Re-run through `WebSearch` + `web_fetch` first, not last. Distinguish *the tool that is blocked* from *the resource that is reachable* — it is faster and cheaper than a founder settings change, and it retires most "allowlist" escalations before they're raised.

6. **The render-is-a-method-not-a-gap rule (IG tiles).** Before treating an asset class as a capability gap (a reason to expand the team), confirm it is not simply an un-banked one-time method. Image rendering read as a Designer bottleneck across two runs; it was a `cairosvg` (then Playwright) recipe, now banked.

7. **The URL-binding migration-clock rule (2026-06-12).** Before adopting any external platform or registration, ask: *does this copy our URL into storage we don't control?* If yes, it is a binding; the migration clock steps up when it fires; sequence the domain decision ahead of it.

8. **The dual-instrument rule (2026-06-16, Vietnam).** A reform's *headline is not its legal instrument*. In one-party-plus-legislature systems a reform usually has two distinct instruments — a Party/executive **decision** (direction) and a legislature's **resolution/law** (budget) — and outlets collapse them. Name both with their own dates; distinguish **adoption** from **signing/certification**; conflate neither. The policy-instrument twin of #4. Companion fence: when a reform "removes a cost," establish what that cost legally *is* and what it is *not* (*tuition-free ≠ fee-free*), sourced to the country's own press.

9. **The measured-vs-announced rule (2026-06-18, Egypt NPDA) — the third dimension of #4.** When one programme reports its scale through several bodies at several magnitudes, they are **different currencies, not the same number**: an *independently-measured* figure (Egypt: UNICEF Innocenti, 180k students, +17%, quasi-experimental) and *announced* figures (programme comms ½m; government 1.38m / 27 governorates). **Lead with the measured one, label the rest "programme-reported," and never sum or average across the seam** — doing so launders a tested number into an untested one. The discriminator (*which figure a system leads with*) is more revealing than any single figure, and is itself the durable take. Companion sub-rule: **date recirculated baseline statistics** — a crisis baseline is often an older measurement recirculated as current (Egypt's "six-in-ten cannot read" is pre-programme learning-poverty data, WB/UNESCO, nearer seven-in-ten post-pandemic). Present it as a standing baseline with its vintage, not a fresh result; give the attributed range when credible vintages disagree.

10. **The right-instrument rule (2026-06-19, Philippines MTB-MLE) — the fourth dimension of #4.** A figure can be correctly read, in its own currency, and *still* be the **wrong instrument** for the claim it is drafted into. The Philippines reading score (PISA 2022 = 347) is real and severe, but it tests **15-year-olds**, in **Filipino/English**, **years after** the disputed K–3 mother-tongue grades — so it can name the crisis but cannot show the policy *caused* it or that ending it *cures* it, even as both camps cite it as proof. Before any figure carries **causal/adjudicating** weight (not merely descriptive), check three matches against the policy: **population, timing, mechanism**. If any fails, the number is descriptive, not dispositive — **say so in print and deny it to both sides as proof.** The number is right; the *inference* is the error. (Distinct from #4 = misreading, #9 = summing across currencies; the three compound — read correctly **and** kept in currency **and** the right instrument.) Companion: on a statutory **reversal**, frame as dispute not verdict, give both camps their strongest honest case, and locate where the question is actually settled (here the law's three-year review → the forward-looking #10 test: *measure the children you just moved*).

11. **The publishable-subset rule (2026-06-20, Oman) — the fifth dimension of #4.** When the primary surface that would settle a figure is **unreachable** *and* the second-hand tallies **don't reconcile**, the number does not run. Publish only the subset that triangulates from reachable primaries (Oman: the 58-school first-phase reach and the single named bottom-band school — **not** the press band-tally that sums to 59 ≠ 58), and **state the withholding in print.** On a transparency story the disclosure is the credibility signal, not an apology. Compounds with #4 (read-it-right) / #9 (currency) / #10 (right-instrument): a figure must clear all four to carry weight. Two companion frames banked the same day: the **adopt-as-the-model-retreats** check (when country X imports country Y's instrument, ask what Y is doing with it *now* — Oman built Ofsted-style single-verdict inspection as England scrapped it, Sep 2024), and the **route-2 conditional-closure** pattern (a candidate banked figure-OPEN + voice-GAP can still ship as an explicit systems piece **iff** the figure is withheld and the voice-gap is named in-piece — Editor's call, on record).

12. **The self-administered-vs-external-assessment rule (2026-06-24, Brazil ICA) — the sixth dimension of #4.** When a system reports its own headline outcome, ask **who held the ruler.** A figure *applied and scored by the same systems it grades* (Brazil's state-administered Indicador Criança Alfabetizada: 66% in 2025) and a figure from an *external assessor* (the federal Saeb: ~49% comparable) are **different currencies** even for the same skill and year — and the ~7-point gap is **the credibility seam, not noise to average away.** Tag every headline figure by administration model (self-administered / externally-administered / programme-announced — ascending independence; the last is #9's *announced* tier); when two disagree, **print both, attribute each, name the gap.** Watch for the instrument *settling under you* (a rising series partly reflects a comparability standard still being hardened — Brazil's INEP *IN 02/2025*), and lead with the **between-unit spread** (Ceará 85.3% vs national 66%), never the mean. Adds **administrator** as a fourth pin to #4 (report · cycle · scope · administrator). Full note: `2026-06-24-self-administered-vs-external-assessment.md`.
   - **#12b — comparability is not independence (2026-06-25, Brazil ship).** Independence and comparability are **two axes, not one**. Ruling #12 ranks figures by administration *independence* (self → external → audited); but the **more independent** measure is **not** automatically the better one to compare units against. Brazil: the federal **Saeb is independent but a sample** (per-state error >10 pts → not unit-comparable), while the state-administered **ICA is a census** on the same external cutoff (unit-comparable but not independent) — so the *self-administered* number is, here, the *more comparable* one. Tag every assessment figure on **both** axes (administration: self/external/audited · coverage: sample/census/moderated); when two disagree, **name which axis explains the gap**, and don't reflexively lead with the more independent one. Full note: `2026-06-25-comparability-is-not-independence.md`.

13. **The frame-sibling-spacing rule (2026-06-25, RAK↔Egypt; founder-flagged).** A **diptych** (two pieces sharing a throughline by design — RAK Iqra trial ↔ Egypt NPDA at national scale) is a strength **only if the two share the throughline and differ in execution.** When they also share the *staging* — near-identical opening move, majority-overlapping sources, twin hero stills, stacked in one edition — they read as one report split in two. At commission the Editor checks a frame-sibling against its nearest kin on three axes: **(1) different entry** (don't re-stage the sibling's opening — lead from a different door: scale, instrument, scene, measurement); **(2) its own durable take** (RAK = meet-the-child-in-her-language; Egypt = measured-vs-announced — if the takeaways collapse, it's one piece); **(3) spacing + visual distinction** (prefer different editions; never twin the stills). Verdict line: *Frame-sibling check: nearest kin = <slug> · entry differs ☐ · own take ☐ · still not a twin ☐ · spacing OK ☐.* Disciplines clusters; does not discourage them. Full note: `2026-06-25-frame-sibling-spacing.md`.
