# Ruling #13 — frame-sibling spacing (cluster pieces share the throughline, not the execution)

**Banked:** 2026-06-25 · **Owner:** Editor (applied at commission) · **Type:** durable editorial ruling
**Paid for by:** the founder's observation (2026-06-25) that the **Egypt NPDA (06-18)** and **Ras al-Khaimah Iqra (06-10)** pieces — both Edition 02 — read as too similar.

---

## The finding

The two pieces are a **deliberate diptych**: RAK is the small randomised trial (does the Arabic-diglossia reading bridge work under controlled conditions?), Egypt is the same question at national scale. The pairing is good — clusters are an established Madār device, and Egypt explicitly picks up RAK's unanswered "does it scale?" question. **The throughline was meant to be shared.**

What was *not* meant to be shared was the **execution**, and that is where they collided:
- near-identical **opening move** (a child arrives at school fluent → opens a book → "not quite hers" → "linguists call it diglossia" → cost paid at reading age);
- three **overlapping sources** (Saiegh-Haddad 2025, the *Frontiers* non-deficit paper, the *Egyptian Streets* explainer);
- a near-**twin hero still** (scatter of spoken sound-marks → kiln-orange bridge → one written word);
- both stacked **inside one edition**, eight days apart.

A diptych works when the two panels share *only* the throughline and differ visibly in everything else. These shared the throughline **and** the staging, so they read as one report split in two rather than two pieces in conversation.

## The rule

**When a new candidate shares a frame with a live piece, it must differ in execution, and frame-siblings should be spaced across editions — not stacked in one.** At commission the Editor checks three things against the sibling:

1. **Different entry.** The opening cannot re-stage the sibling's opening. Lead from a different door — scale, instrument, a named scene, the measurement — so the reader is not re-taught what the first piece already taught. (Fix applied 06-25: Egypt's build-up was re-cut to lead on *scale* — "a hundred million speakers" — and to turn the RAK link into the analytical hook, not a re-run of the child-opens-a-book tableau; the shared non-deficit aphorism was de-twinned.)
2. **Distinct durable take.** Each piece must carry its *own* transferable lesson (RAK: *meet the child in the language she has / engineering-not-deficit*; Egypt: *measured-vs-announced, don't sum the currencies*). If the takeaways collapse into one, it is one piece, not two.
3. **Spacing + visual distinction.** Prefer siblings in **different editions**; if they must share one, the **hero stills must not be twins**, and the source overlap should be visible but not majority.

## How to apply
- The Researcher flags frame-adjacency at recon ("this rhymes with <live slug> — differentiate entry, take, still").
- The Editor's verdict carries a line: *Frame-sibling check: nearest kin = <slug> · entry differs ☐ · own durable take ☐ · still not a twin ☐ · spacing OK ☐.*
- This does **not** discourage clusters — the related-reading rails and reading-paths are a strength. It disciplines *how* a cluster is built so it reads as a set, not as repetition.
