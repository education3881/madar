**Subject:** The Philippines has legally dismantled its flagship mother-tongue-based multilingual education (MTB-MLE) policy — a contested reversal that rhymes with Madār's language-of-instruction thread (Arabic diglossia) and with the Korea policy-reversal frame.

## Why now (2024–2026)
**RA 12027** (lapsed into law **10 Oct 2024**) ends the *mandatory* use of the mother tongue as medium of instruction K–Grade 3, reverting to Filipino + English (L1 now optional only in qualifying "monolingual classes"). **DepEd Order No. 20, s. 2025** operationalises it for **SY 2025–2026** (nationwide K–3); a KWF language-mapping policy was due Oct 2025 governing full Key-Stage-1 rollout in **SY 2026–2027**. Rolling out now.

## Instruments (verified to resolve 2026-06-16)
- **RA 10533** Enhanced Basic Education Act 2013 — the original MTB-MLE mandate.
- **DepEd Order No. 24, s. 2023** — MATATAG K–10 curriculum (10 Aug 2023); dropped Mother Tongue as a distinct *subject*.
- **RA 12027** (10 Oct 2024) — discontinues MT as mandatory *medium*, K–3; amends RA 10533. Full text: lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2024/ra_12027_2024.html (verified; cite lawphil — the Official Gazette mirror is JS-gated).
- **DepEd Order No. 20, s. 2025** + IRR **DM 059, s. 2025** (Aug 2025).
- **Correction to carry:** RA 11476 is the GMRC/Values Education Act (2020) — **NOT** the MTB instrument. The reversal runs DO 24 s.2023 → RA 12027.
