# Guidebook — the evaluator-on-record tier (what to do when the primary report isn't public yet)

**Banked:** 2026-06-10 · **Owner:** Editor (ruling made in the Iqra/diglossia verdict of the same date)
**Generalises:** the source-hierarchy rules of 2026-06-08 (egress wall / primary-not-blog) and 2026-06-09 (two-studies-same-name trap).

## The situation this ruling covers

A program has been independently evaluated; the press reports the findings; the operator confirms them; **but the evaluator's standalone results report is not yet published.** The Iqra case (J-PAL MENA, 2024–25, Ras al-Khaimah) is the type specimen: figures (≈25% faster progress; median 50th→54th; 50th→58th word reading; 83 classrooms / 41-vs-42 design) appear in May 2026 press coverage and in the foundation's announcement — and nowhere else yet.

The naive readings are both wrong:
- **"It's in three newspapers, so it's sourced"** — no. Press corroboration is replication of a press release, not of a finding.
- **"No primary PDF, so park it"** — also no, *if* something categorically better than paraphrase exists.

## The ruling — a tier between "primary in hand" and "press paraphrase"

A figure may run **without the primary report in hand** only when ALL of the following hold:

1. **The evaluator is on the record, by name, with institutional affiliation,** describing the finding (Iqra: Nayera Adly Husseiny, J-PAL MENA, quoted directly on the evaluation's result).
2. **The operator of record confirms separately, by name** (Iqra: Hanadi Mohammed and Dr Natasha Ridge, Al Qasimi Foundation).
3. **The trial design is stated specifically enough to be falsifiable** (sample, arms, period: 83 classrooms, 41/42, 2024–25). Vague "a study found" never clears.
4. **Every figure carries in-text attribution to the named evaluation** — never asserted in Madār's own voice. The reader can see whose number it is in the sentence itself.
5. **A standing follow-up is logged:** when the report publishes, add it to `sources:` and re-verify each figure against it. The piece is not "done sourcing"; it is sourced to the best tier that exists, with a debt recorded.

If any of the five fails, the old rule stands: trace to the primary or park.

## Why this is the honest line (and not a loosened bar)

The bar's purpose is to never launder paraphrase into fact. A named evaluator describing her own randomised trial, datelined and quoted, *is* a primary source — a person, not a PDF. What the missing report withholds is *detail* (standard errors, attrition, subgroups), and the piece must therefore not assert detail beyond what the on-record statements carry — which is also why the Iqra piece states effect sizes plainly ("real and modest", "four percentiles") instead of dressing them up.

## Checklist line for future verdicts

> Trace tier: ☐ primary report in hand · ☐ evaluator-on-record (5 conditions verified, follow-up logged) · ☐ neither → park.
