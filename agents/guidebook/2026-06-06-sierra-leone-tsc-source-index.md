## The hard figures (2024 deployment round) — each must be traced to a primary before it enters a draft

2,341 teachers recruited · 100% of selected teachers passed the licensing exam · 64% deployed to schools with pupil-to-payroll-teacher ratio >100 · 57% to disadvantaged districts · 54% matched to preferred school · 7% placed >5 km from preferred option · female-teacher tiebreaker rule. **Source the numbers from the technical report (DOI .1115), not the blog summary.**

## The limits (mandatory counter-texture — on the record)

Too few candidates passed the licensing exam to fill the intended pool · TMIS↔school-census data-integration gaps · limited TSC digital capacity · weak initial teacher communication about the matching logic.

## Method note for the team (the transferable lesson)

The Saudi candidate parked because no named operational voice at the classroom tier carried a recent dateline. Sierra Leone cleared because we looked for the *operational* tier (a TSC director on classroom impact, Dec 2024) instead of the *ceremonial* tier (a launch quote). **The reusable rule: when a strategy PDF is the candidate, the unpark hinges on finding the person who runs the mechanism, datelined 2024+, not the person who announced it.** Apply this to every future "document worth reading slowly" candidate before commissioning.
