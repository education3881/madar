# Guidebook increment — Saudi early-childhood (ECE) sourcing: primary-source index + a transliteration ruling

**Date:** 2026-06-04 · **Logged by:** Arabic Editor (with the Editor) · **Type:** verified primary-source index entry + style ruling
**Why this exists:** the team has now attempted the Saudi 17 → 90 kindergarten story **twice** (2026-05-30 recon, 2026-06-03 follow-up) and run a dedicated Arabic-original pass (2026-06-04). Rather than re-walk the same dead ends a third time, this entry fixes what is known about the source landscape so the next writer starts where we left off.

## The structural finding (carry this forward)

Saudi ECE has a **tiered sourcing asymmetry**: the *strategic* tier is rich and quotable; the *operational/classroom* tier is not reachable in open primaries — **in either Arabic or English.** This is now confirmed across two languages and three sessions. The lesson generalises: for a government-led program with a flagship national target, expect the minister and the target to be over-documented and the classroom to be under-documented. Plan the sourcing around that asymmetry from the start, instead of discovering it on day three.

## The route that would actually clear the bar

A **data-led inversion**: let the GASTAT 2025 report (and the verbatim ministerial target) carry the why-now, and require only **one** named operational voice (a KG director or supervisor, datelined 2024+) to carry the dignity. This lowers the operational-sourcing burden from "build the piece on a classroom voice" to "anchor a data-and-strategy piece with a single human," which is reachable. Try this framing before the next from-scratch attempt.

## Transliteration ruling (Arabic Editor — binding)

The named administrator, should she appear in any future EN piece:
- Arabic: **حصة بنت عبد العزيز الفارس**
- English: **Hessa bint Abdulaziz Al-Faris** (not "Hissa", not "Al-Fares"). Title in EN: *Director of the Kindergarten Department, Riyadh Region (Ministry of Education)* — at the date of the 2018 statement; re-verify the title before using, as regional postings rotate.
- Minister: Arabic **يوسف بن عبد الله البنيان** → English **Yousef bin Abdullah Al-Benyan** (keep "Al-Benyan"; do not render "Benyan" bare). Re-verify he still holds the education portfolio at time of publication.

General rule reaffirmed: for Saudi names, default to the **government's own English romanisation** when the body (SPA, MOE, GASTAT) publishes one in English; only fall back to our transliteration when no official English spelling exists.
