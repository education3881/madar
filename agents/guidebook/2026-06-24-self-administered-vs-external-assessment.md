# The self-administered-vs-external-assessment rule (Brazil ICA) — ruling #12, sixth dimension of #4

**Logged:** 2026-06-24 (Brazil *Indicador Criança Alfabetizada* recon) · **Owner of application:** Researcher at recon, Editor at verdict.

## The rule

When a system reports its own headline outcome, ask **who administered the measurement**. A number applied and scored by the *same systems it grades* (a self-administered indicator) and a number applied by an *external assessor* (a federal/independent exam) are **different currencies** — even when they nominally measure "the same thing" for "the same year." The gap between them is **not noise to be averaged away; it is the credibility seam, and usually the story.**

This is the **sixth dimension of ruling #4** ("a program name is not a citation — pin every figure to its report, cycle, and scope"). The earlier dimensions pinned *which report, which cycle, which scope*. This one pins **who held the ruler**.

## The worked case

- Brazil's **Indicador Criança Alfabetizada (ICA)** — INEP supplies the items, **the states administer and score**. National: 56% (2023) → 59.2% (2024) → 66% (2025). This is the number that travels in every headline and ceremony.
- The federal **Saeb** — externally administered — read ~**49%** for the comparable period. MEC's public line: the federal exam "is not the indicator."
- Same country, same skill, same era; **~7 points apart** because **the administration model differs.** Lead with the ICA uncritically and you launder a self-graded number into an audited one.

## How to apply (at recon and in print)

1. **Tag every headline figure with its administration model:** self-administered / externally-administered / programme-announced (the last is ruling #9's *announced* tier). Three rungs, ascending in independence.
2. **When a self-administered figure and an external one disagree, print both,** attribute each to its administrator, and **name the gap** rather than reconciling or averaging it. ("The states grade 66%; the federal exam reads lower; the distance is the instrument, not the children.")
3. **Watch for the instrument settling under you.** If the assessor is *still hardening comparability* (Brazil: INEP *IN nº 02/2025* exists to strengthen 2nd-year comparability across states), then a rising series partly reflects a maturing ruler. Say so.
4. **Spread > mean.** A self-administered national mean often hides a wide between-unit spread (Ceará 85.3% vs national 66%). Lead with the spread; the mean is the least informative cut.

## Relationship to the existing rulings

- **#4** (pin report/cycle/scope) → this adds **administrator** as a fourth pin.
- **#9** (measured-vs-announced) → adjacent but distinct: #9 separates *measured* from *PR-announced*; #12 separates **self-measured** from **externally-measured** *within* the measured tier. Order of trust: external audit > self-administered indicator > programme announcement.
- **#11** (publish what you can read) → the disclosure move is the same: when instruments disagree, *the disclosure of the disagreement is the credibility signal.*

**One-line:** *Two numbers for the same thing usually means two rulers — name who held each, and report the gap as the finding.*
