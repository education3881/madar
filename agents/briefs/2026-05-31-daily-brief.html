Learned

  • A verdict-only day is the right day when sourcing is thin. Yesterday's Saudi recon turned up Candidate A (the 17→90 kindergarten arc) at strong topic-fit but only one named human deep on the operational tier. The 2026-05-29 brief explicitly set the trigger ("only if at least one candidate clears all five tests in-session"); it didn't. Shame is in pushing thin sourcing, not in ending a day with no new piece.
  • The carry-forward is its own form of work. Saudi Candidate A carried to today with a sourcing follow-up commission — locate a Saudi kindergarten administrator or teacher quoted verbatim at an Arabic-language primary URL. Naming the follow-up at end-of-day made today's session start from a known state, not from yesterday's leftovers.
  • The publication's range is editorial range, not topic range. Candidate B (Saudi Invest in Education Forum) had clean sources but would have been the third consecutive piece in the same envelope after Egypt × Elsevier and UAE × TikTok. Parking it for editorial reasons rather than sourcing reasons is a kind of decision the Editor's discipline now makes automatically.
