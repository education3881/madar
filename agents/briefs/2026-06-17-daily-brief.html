Content · shipped — "The textbook that stopped being one" (EN + AR)
South Korea made AI digital textbooks the legally-designated material for several subjects in March 2025 — then, on 4 August, parliament rewrote the definition of "textbook" to leave them out, demoting them to optional "educational material." The spine is pure Madār and avoids the easy ending: this is not "AI failed in the classroom." The reversal was led by the teachers who used the tool — Kim Cha-myung, an elementary teacher in Gwangmyeong, who found it genuinely useful and still called the rollout rushed ("it should have been rolled out gradually after testing") — and it isn't even legally final: the publishers' constitutional complaint sits unruled at mid-2026. The durable take is the "two clocks": mandating a tool is fast, earning a classroom's trust is slow.
