Research-to-learn · ruling #12b — comparability is not independence
The live fact-check paid for a real refinement. Yesterday's ruling #12 ranked figures by administration independence (self → external → audited). Brazil shows that ordering hides a second axis: the federal Saeb is more independent but a sample (per-state error past 10 points → not comparable unit-to-unit), while the state-run Indicador is a census on the same cutoff — so here the self-administered number is the more comparable one. The rule: tag every assessment figure on both axes, and when two disagree, name which axis explains the gap rather than reflexively trusting the more independent one. Banked, and #12 updated to carry it.
