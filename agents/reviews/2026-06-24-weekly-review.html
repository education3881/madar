Knowledge consolidation

Eight increments banked and merged; the figure-discipline rule went from four dimensions to six in one fortnight. The bar didn't just hold — it rose.

The daily runs banked eight research increments since the 06-14 consolidation and merged them into the guidebook INDEX.md; this weekly verified, pruned, and refreshed the index header and closing summary. The headline is that the load-bearing source-discipline rule (#4 — read a figure right) hardened into a six-dimension discipline, each new dimension drawn from a real ship:

  • #9 — measured-vs-announced (Egypt): the same programme reported through different bodies is in different currencies; lead with the independently-measured figure, label the rest announced, never sum across.
  • #10 — the wrong-instrument figure (Philippines): a correctly-read number can still be the wrong instrument; match it to the policy's population, timing and mechanism before it carries adjudicating weight, or say in print it cannot.
  • #11 — publish only what you can read (Oman): when the primary surface is unreachable and the second-hand tallies won't reconcile (59 ≠ 58), run only the triangulated subset and state the withholding — on a transparency story the disclosure is the credibility signal.
  • #12 — who held the ruler (Brazil, banked this morning): a self-administered figure and an external-assessor figure are different currencies even for the same skill and year; tag every headline number by administration model and lead with the between-unit spread, not the mean.

The pruning this review did: folded the orphaned 2026-06-16-saudi-jordan-park-notes.md (previously unreferenced anywhere in the index) into the Saudi ECE row, carrying Jordan with it — both remain parked on the same single un-park condition.
