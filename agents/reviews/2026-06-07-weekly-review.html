Section 03

Knowledge consolidation

Four loose increments this week, no duplicates — consolidated into a durable index with reusable rulings.

The week produced four research increments: the Saudi ECE source index (06-04), the render-stills-to-PNG method (06-05), the Sierra Leone TSC source index (06-06), and the egress-boundary sourcing method (06-07). They are distinct and durable — there were no duplicates to prune. Consolidation took two forms: I created agents/guidebook/INDEX.md as a single entry point cataloguing all durable working knowledge, and I lifted the transferable rulings out of their country-specific notes so they bind generally:

  • Operational-not-ceremonial voice. When a strategy PDF is the candidate, the unpark hinges on the person who runs the mechanism (datelined 2024+), not the one who announced it. This is exactly why Sierra Leone cleared and Saudi did not.
  • Tiered sourcing asymmetry. Government flagship programs over-document the minister and the target, under-document the classroom — in both languages. Plan for it from day one; consider a data-led inversion before abandoning a candidate.
  • Render is a method, not a gap. Confirm an asset class isn't just an un-banked recipe before escalating it to a capability gap — the lesson that directly informs Section 04.
  • Egress-boundary sourcing. When the primary host is outside the allowlist, search corroborates the spine but cannot trace a figure — quarantine untraceable numbers as [[TRACE]] up front; never let them reach a draft. This surfaced today on the Sierra Leone dossier and converts to a one-time founder unblock (see §1 and the push follow-up): allowlist the source hosts. It is the sourcing-side twin of the @astrojs/sitemap item — infrastructural, not editorial.

The durable HTML manual was not revised this week; nothing changed in the replicable model, only in Madār's own working knowledge. The team is measurably faster on Sierra Leone and Saudi topics than a week ago: the next writer starts from a checked index, not a blank source hunt.
