The one defect that reached production — and the rule that now prevents it. On 06-10 a founder push (feb98b1) landed mid-run; its git add -A swept the two finished IQRA article files to origin ahead of their hero still, leaving the live IQRA pages referencing a still that 404'd until the completion commit caught up. This is a genuine near-miss that briefly bit production — the invariant "no article live without its hero" was momentarily violated. The 06-10 gate caught and corrected it the same run, but it was previously only in memory, not in the operating rules. I have codified it into the RUNBOOK: an article's assets ship in the same commit as its text (write the still to disk immediately after the article, never as a follow-up), and the run re-checks git fetch origin right before composing the push block, because a founder push can land at any moment. The push block stays a single line for your convenience — the fix is the ordering of writes within the run, not pathspec gymnastics.

Carried environmental friction (not a product defect): the node_modules/.vite FUSE boundary still blocks in-place builds every run; routed around via /tmp, documented, not fought. Standing infra item: the hand-built sitemap.xml remains hand-maintained — it held this week (no hand-edit needed after the page-set stabilised at 27), but the @astrojs/sitemap migration is now folded into the domain cutover so the URL-set change happens once, not twice. Per the charter a missed day earns one clean recovery, never a forced double-batch; the operation resumes on the normal cadence, and this review is the week's accountability for it.

Knowledge consolidation

Six increments banked since the last consolidation; merged, pruned, and one stale founder ask retired.

The week produced six research increments, now consolidated into the guidebook INDEX.md: the egress-wall-is-bash-only finding (06-08), the Arabic-diglossia source index (06-09), the evaluator-on-record trace tier (06-10), the brand-fonts-via-npm render method (06-11), the Korea AIDT source index (06-11), and the URL-binding migration playbook (06-12). The headline is that the team's source discipline hardened from a single rule ("source the report, not the blog") into a four-part framework:

  • The evaluator-on-record trace tier — the load-bearing new ruling. A figure may run without the primary report in hand only when five conditions hold (named evaluator on record + named operator confirmation + falsifiable design + in-text attribution + a logged follow-up). It is what let the IQRA piece ship honestly before J-PAL's report published, without loosening the bar — a named evaluator describing her own randomised trial is a primary source.
  • "A program name is not a citation" — I merged the two near-duplicate rulings (the two-studies-same-name trap from IQRA, the same-figure-different-scope trap from Korea) into one rule: pin every figure to its report, cycle, and scope; state the discriminator in print next to the number.
  • The blocked-tool-is-not-the-unreachable-resource rule — a failed curl is a statement about bash, not the open web; exhaust the web tools first.
  • The text-bearing render rule — line-art via cairosvg, but any real text, especially Arabic, via Playwright/Chromium (which shapes Arabic correctly), fonts from npm not GitHub releases.

The pruning that matters most: I retired the "founder must allowlist edtechhub.org / documents1.worldbank.org" ask. The 06-07 note that raised it was superseded on 06-08, when the same figures traced in full via the web tools and the Sierra Leone piece shipped — yet the ask was erroneously re-listed as a live Vini action in the 06-12 status. It is now corrected at the source of record. The team is measurably more capable than a week ago: three of the four catalogued source indices now carry a closed figure-trace, and the Researcher (Section 04) inherits a clean, current Section 1.

Section 04

Team-expansion decision

The first deliberate hire since the founding seven. Decided against the ladder, with the trigger documented.

Decision: add the Researcher (persona 08). Team grows from seven to eight.

For three weeks I have held the team at seven and recorded why. This week the first rung of the expansion ladder — Researcher — meets the charter's two-part bar (recurring gap AND genuine saturation), and the watch signal I pre-registered in last week's review fired exactly as written. Setting a test and then moving the goalposts when it triggers is the failure mode of indecision; I committed to the test, so I act.

The trigger, on the evidence:

  • The gap is recurring and has become a distinct workstream. Three verified source indices were banked in seven days (Sierra Leone, Arabic diglossia, Korea), each a multi-hour artefact with its own method — the operational-voice gate, the evaluator-on-record trace tier, the same-name and same-scope fences. When a body of method that specific accumulates around one activity, the activity has become a role, not a task.
  • The pre-registered signal fired. Last week's review said: re-test if "the next strategy-PDF candidate after Sierra Leone repeatedly stalls in recon rather than drafting." Korea was exactly that candidate, and it stalled in a heavy archive pass and then parked at the voice gate. Vietnam is now in recon behind it. Two consecutive strategy-PDF candidates routing through deep reconnaissance is the pattern the rung exists for.
  • The Editor is the bottleneck the charter names. The charter's primary trigger is "the Editor repeatedly bottlenecked doing recon before drafting." That is met independent of any throughput question — and on 06-12, two recon-class workloads in one day consumed the entire content capacity.

The honest counter-argument, and why I add anyway. Part of 06-12's saturation was inflated by two one-off founder asks (the domain memo and the Issues-page work) landing the same day as Korea recon — so I do not lean on saturation alone. I lean on the recurring-gap half, which is three weeks old and independent of any single day's founder burst. And I lean on the charter's deeper logic: the Researcher protects quality-over-slot rather than threatening it. Its job is to make the park-or-proceed call earlier and on better evidence, so the Editor stays a pure filter and recon stops eating the same day's content slot. It is the "recon precedes scope commitment" discipline — already proven in the override-mode finding — made into a standing function. The role is scoped to reconnaissance, explicitly not to throughput: adding it does not raise the cadence.

What I did to wire it in: scaffolded /agents/08_researcher.md in the house persona format (reports to the Editor; produces the source index + the operational-voice gate call before the Editor commits a piece to commission; does not write drafts or render verdicts); updated the RUNBOOK routing diagram (Editor → {Researcher, Content Creator}); handed it ownership of Section 1 of the guidebook INDEX; and briefed its first week — take over the live Vietnam recon, run the operational-voice gate on the next candidate before commission, and bank one durable ruling. The rungs below it stay closed: Social/Video (render resolved to a method, not a gap), Data/SEO analyst (premature until there is a traffic surface — re-test after the domain cutover + first send), second-language editor (no third language on the roadmap).
