## The job, in one line

The opening grid is nine tiles across three weeks. Seven reuse stills already published on the site — your job there is a clean 1080×1080 PNG export, not new design. Two tiles do not exist yet and need to be made: **Tile 5 (wordmark on paper)** and **Tile 8 (the macron-rule divider).**

## The two new tiles

### Tile 5 — Wordmark on paper

The publication speaking as itself, at the centre of the grid.

- **Canvas:** 1080×1080, paper background `#FAFAF7` with the same faint fractal-noise multiply texture used site-wide (opacity ~0.035). If the texture is fussy to export at this size, a flat `#FAFAF7` is acceptable — the wordmark carries the tile.
- **Mark:** `Madār·مدار` centred, in Cormorant Garamond 500. The macron dot between the Latin and Arabic forms is `#D94F2A` (orange); everything else is ink `#0E1B2C`. Source the lockup from `/design-assets/wordmark/madar-wordmark.svg`.
- **Scale:** wordmark occupies roughly the central 60% width, generous paper margin all around. This tile is a breath, not a billboard. Restraint reads as confidence.
- **No tagline, no URL, no decoration.** The mark alone.

### Tile 8 — Macron-rule divider

The quiet structural mark of the grid's bottom row — a visual rest between the two range pieces (Ghana on its left, Uruguay on its right).

- **Canvas:** 1080×1080, same paper + optional texture as Tile 5.
- **Mark:** a single horizontal hairline rule, centred vertically, in ink at the site's `--color-rule` weight (rgba ink at ~0.18, ~1px scaled to the 1080 canvas). Centred over the rule, one short macron stroke or dot in orange `#D94F2A` — echoing the `·` in the wordmark. The rule spans roughly the central 70% width, not edge to edge.
- **Intent:** this is the print-rule motif from the article layouts, lifted to a tile. It should read as the same hand that set the type. Nothing else on the canvas.
