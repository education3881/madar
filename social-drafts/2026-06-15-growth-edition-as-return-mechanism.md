# Growth — the Edition is the return mechanism (terminology reset + Edition 02 positioning)

**Date:** 2026-06-15 · **Owner:** Growth · **Standing frame:** lead with *return rate*, not raw counts. No third-party tracker by design.

## The strategic read (why today's ship matters to Growth)

The founder shipped the **editions-home + covers** feature today (`86c4478`, live). This is the **single biggest return-rate lever we have built**, and Growth should treat it that way:

- A slow-cadence publication's audience problem is not *acquisition*, it's *return*. With no tracker, our honest north star is **"did a reader who came once come back."**
- The Editions model gives a reader a **reason and a rhythm to return**: one new edition a month, each with its own colour, cover, and editorial question, plus an archive that rewards a second visit. The homepage now *leads* with the current edition — so a returning reader immediately sees "what's new this month," not a static list.
- Translation for Growth: **stop optimising for the one-time open; optimise for the monthly comeback.** Every distribution action should point at *the edition*, not just *a piece*.
