---

## What this list is for

The persona names a "starter list of ~20 education community members worth following / quietly engaging with once the publication launches." Quiet following has three operational uses:

1. **Signal intake.** When one of these accounts publishes something, the Editor sees it. Some of those signals will surface themes for future issues.
2. **First-share readiness.** When one of these accounts mentions us in the open (a thoughtful reply, a quoted quote, a citation), we know to thank them once, briefly, in their original channel — per persona rules.
3. **Verifiable reference.** Each entry below ties to a public, verifiable source. We don't fabricate handles. Where I haven't confirmed a person's social presence, I list the institution + role + reference, and flag handle "TO VERIFY AT ENGAGEMENT TIME." The publication's sourcing discipline applies to its own contact list.

---

## How this list is structured

Each entry: **Name · Role / Institution · Why · Reference · Channel(s) (to verify on first follow).**

The list is sorted by category: researchers (1–6), journalists/editors (7–11), policy & multilateral organisation people (12–15), institutions worth following at the org level (16–20). The split is roughly 30/25/20/25 between research / press / policy / institutional.

The geographies covered map to our editorial spine, with weight on MENA + Africa. Per the geography-internal rule, this list is internal and never appears in public copy.

## What this list does NOT contain (and why)

- **No generic "education influencers."** People who do volume on LinkedIn or X without a primary-source publication record are off the list. The publication does not learn from them.
- **No edtech-vendor accounts.** Vendor marketing surfaces are not where the publication's editorial signals live.
- **No teachers' union accounts as such.** Worth reading; not worth following as a signal-intake list. Specific labour-side researchers, yes; generic union channels, no.
- **No "thought-leadership" Substacks.** The publication is going on Substack; that does not mean it reads other Substacks as a peer set. The peer set is research, primary-source journalism, and named field practitioners.

## Cadence for engagement (per persona)

- **Follow on day one of the IG account being live.** The list of 20 above. No "follow for follow" expectation. Some of these accounts will never engage back; the follow is for signal-intake, not reciprocity.
- **Read consistently for two months.** During that period, do nothing. No comments, no DMs, no "great piece" replies. Read.
- **Engage only after they have engaged with us.** If one of these accounts shares a Madār piece in the open, the publication thanks them once, briefly, in the original channel. If one of them replies to a piece substantively, the publication takes the reply seriously and responds.
- **Re-curate the list every two months.** Drop accounts that have gone quiet or off-register. Add accounts that have surfaced through inbound mentions. The list is operational, not aspirational.
