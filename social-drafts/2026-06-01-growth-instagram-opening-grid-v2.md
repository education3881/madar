## What this document is

The 2026-05-26 opening-grid plan was built around two pieces (Sierra Leone + Bahrain). The catalogue is now ten. This v2 redesigns the grid so a first-time profile visitor sees the publication's range, posture, and bilingual claim in nine tiles — read in the order Instagram's grid actually renders them (top-left = most recent post).

The grid is posted in **reverse** of the order below: Post 9 first, then 8, then 7… so that Post 1 (the Sierra Leone tile) ends up top-left.

---

## Posture rules carried over from the persona (binding)

- ≤ 220 characters per caption.
- No engagement bait.
- Max 5 hashtags, specific, never generic. Never hashtag a geography (the country-list-is-internal rule applies on social copy).
- No "did you know" openers, no rhetorical questions, no double-tap-if-you-agree.
- Caption ends with the URL to the piece, or `Read it — link in bio.` if URL doesn't render cleanly in Instagram captions (Instagram doesn't hyperlink in captions; the URL is for the reader's eye, not their click).
- Where a piece has both EN and AR live, the caption acknowledges this in the close.
- No emoji. No flags. No "🚀".

---

## The grid — nine tiles, posted in reverse order

Top-left of the rendered profile = Tile 1. To get Tile 1 in the top-left, post the tiles in order **9 → 8 → 7 → 6 → 5 → 4 → 3 → 2 → 1**, three posts a week (Monday / Wednesday / Friday, 09:00 Gulf time), so the grid lands in roughly three weeks. The whole sequence is the publication's first three weeks on Instagram.

The grid reads, top-row to bottom-row in the final state, as:

```
[ 1 — Sierra Leone ]   [ 2 — Bahrain ]        [ 3 — Egypt ]
[ 4 — UAE Madrasa ]    [ 5 — Wordmark ]       [ 6 — Iraq Mosul ]
[ 7 — Ghana ]          [ 8 — Quiet rule ]     [ 9 — Uruguay ]
```

The centre tile (5) is the publication's wordmark on `--color-paper`. The centre-bottom tile (8) is the macron-rule divider — a deliberately quiet tile. The other seven tiles are pieces, chosen so the grid covers the publication's three regions and both languages.

Pieces deliberately **not** in the opening grid: Kuwait CCET, Indonesia Permen 13, Qatar Strategy. Reasons: Kuwait sits well with CCET's longer-form treatment but the still is quieter than Bahrain's and Bahrain represents the GCC slot more legibly at thumbnail size; Indonesia and Qatar are recent and can land as posts 10–11 once the grid has stabilised. These three feed the second-week sequence.
