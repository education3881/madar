## Why this piece is a return-rate asset (the growth read)

A slow publication's growth problem is the **second visit**, not the first. This piece is built for it. "They made school free — except the part that costs money" is a frame a reader *remembers* and *re-uses*: it reframes every "free education" headline they'll see next. That is what brings someone back to the archive and makes them forward it — not volume. The piece also widens the map to **Asia** (only our second), which is the discoverability argument below: it lets Madār surface on a query cluster the MENA corpus never could.

> Vietnam waived tuition for 23 million children. Tuition was the smallest line in the family budget. The fees that actually keep a child home — meals, insurance, the "voluntary" fund — are untouched. *Tuition-free is true. Fee-free is not.*
