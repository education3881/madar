## Why this piece is a return-rate asset (the growth read)

A slow publication's growth problem is the **second visit**. This piece is built for it on two fronts.

1. **A frame the reader re-uses.** "A country's literacy number rose to 66% — graded by the same school systems it measures; the independent exam reads 49%; a serious state published both" is a lens a reader carries to *every* official statistic they meet next. That re-use — not volume — is what brings someone back to the archive and makes them forward it.
2. **It opens Latin America.** Brazil is only the corpus's **second LatAm piece** (after Uruguay) and the **first in Edition 02**. That widens the map onto a query cluster the MENA-heavy corpus can't reach — the discoverability argument below.

The strongest distribution line is the spine, stated plainly:

> Brazil made one state's literacy model national and pinned a single number to it. It rose three years running, to 66%. But that number is scored by the same school systems it grades — and the government's *own* external exam reads 49%. The honest part isn't the 66%. It's that Brazil printed the 49% next to it.
