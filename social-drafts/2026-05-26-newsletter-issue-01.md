**This issue's lead — Sierra Leone**

*The teacher in Bo who still uses last term's chalk*

Sierra Leone's Free Quality School Education policy turned ten in 2026. The piece argues that the policy is, by most reasonable measures, one of the more consequential domestic education reforms on the African continent in the last fifteen years — and that its success has been quietly subsidized by the people it most relied on, in the form of unpaid hours, personal expenditure on classroom materials, and a professional status that has not risen with the policy's profile. Both things are true. Neither cancels the other.

The piece names a specific place (Bo, the country's second city), names a specific institution (the Free Quality School Education policy and the Teaching Service Commission's 2022 salary scale), and uses one composite character — disclosed on the page with a dagger — assembled from interviews and ministry documents.

Read it: **education3881.github.io/madar/articles/2026-05-25-bo-teacher-chalk/**

**Why this publication exists**

There is a kind of education writing we read often: aggregated, fast, agency-framed, written in a register that flattens specific schools into "stakeholders" and specific teachers into "voices." There is another kind we read rarely: writing that names a specific place, a specific time, and at least one named human being whose presence is not decorative; writing that cites primary sources with working links; writing that admits what it does not know.

Madār is an attempt at the second kind, in two languages.

We are not a magazine of news, and not an aggregator. A reader who comes for the speed of a feed is in the wrong place; a reader who wants to know what a primary teacher actually does on Monday morning, in a named school, in a named country, is in the right one.

If that's a publication you want to read, we are glad you're here.

## What this newsletter deliberately does NOT do

Listed so future drafts don't drift:

- It does not enumerate the publication's geographic scope. Per the strict editorial spine, the country list is internal only. The publication's posture is signaled through what it covers, never by naming what it doesn't.
- It does not use marketing-register subject lines (no "Welcome to…!", no exclamation marks, no "Issue 01 is HERE!").
- It does not promise a fixed cadence beyond "one issue a month, plus the occasional short note." We will not over-promise the schedule.
- It does not quote from documents whose existence cannot be verified. Jordan and Colombia pieces are not amplified in this email until the sourcing question is resolved.
- It does not include a tracking pixel, a referral-link incentive, a "forward to a friend who needs to read this" social hook, or anything else that turns the newsletter into a growth instrument. The publication grows by being read, not by being amplified.
