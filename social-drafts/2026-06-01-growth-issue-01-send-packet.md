## 1. Issue 01 — three editorial options for the Editor to choose between

Growth does not decide content. Three coherent options are on the table; the Editor picks one and that becomes the send. Each carries a recommended subject line drafted in the publication's voice (the persona rules: specific, never a question, never a number).

### Option A — *Founding restraint* (Growth's recommendation)

**Pieces:** Sierra Leone (Bo teacher) + Bahrain (BQA public grading).
**Through-line:** *Two ways a state can take responsibility for what a child is being taught.* Sierra Leone has paid for the policy; Bahrain has named the schools.
**Why it's the recommendation:** the founding newsletter is a posture statement. Slow is the posture. A two-piece Issue 01 lands the publication's cadence promise on send-day-one. Future issues earn the right to feature more.
**Subject line draft:** *Two pieces, slowly — Issue 01.*

### Option B — *Public-good infrastructure at ten*

**Pieces:** Sierra Leone (FQSE at 10) + Egypt (EKB at 10) + Bahrain (BQA's 2024–25 posture as present-tense companion).
**Through-line:** *Two ten-year-old public-education-infrastructure projects, and one regulator deciding what a public report can hold.*
**Why this works:** the editorial spine is legible — two anniversaries and a present-tense witness — and it makes the publication's geographic posture visible without listing geographies.
**Risk:** three pieces is heavier than the cadence promise wants the first send to be.
**Subject line draft:** *What ten years bought — Issue 01.*

### Option C — *GCC arc*

**Pieces:** Bahrain (BQA) + UAE (Madrasa × TikTok) + Qatar (Education Strategy 2024–30) + Kuwait (CCET).
**Through-line:** *Four GCC states, four different routes to a public commitment in education.*
**Why an Editor might pick this:** the GCC priority cluster set on 2026-05-25 deserves a single coherent surface, and four pieces in four GCC countries is that surface.
**Risk:** loses the publication's range posture; reads as a regional newsletter, not the publication. Four pieces also strains the cadence promise on send one.
**Subject line draft:** *Four states, four routes — Issue 01.*

**Growth's pick: A.** The founding issue is a posture statement and restraint is the posture. If the Editor disagrees, the alternative is B (one extra piece, defensible through-line). C reads as a regional issue and should be queued for a later send when the publication has earned the range to do a themed issue.

## 4. The 25-minute Vini checklist

This is the entire surface of Vini's involvement. Open this section side-by-side with substack.com on a desktop browser. Twenty-five minutes, one sitting, end-to-end.

**Pre-flight (1 minute):** Have these four assets in browser tabs:

- `/design-assets/wordmark/madar-wordmark.svg` (the wordmark — Substack profile photo)
- `/web/dist/stills/2026-05-25-bo-teacher-chalk.svg` exported as 1080×1080 PNG (Substack post header image, Sierra Leone)
- `/web/dist/stills/2026-05-26-bahrain-bqa-public-grades.svg` exported as 1080×1080 PNG (Substack post header image, Bahrain — optional second image)
- This document open in a third tab so you can copy-paste blocks

**Step 1 — Create the publication (5 minutes):**

1. Open https://substack.com/signup. Sign up with `education3881@gmail.com`. Confirm via the email Substack sends.
2. Publication name: `Madār · مدار`. Subdomain: `madar.substack.com` (fallback order: `madarpublication` → `madareditorial` → `madarread`).
3. Subtitle field: `Education, written slowly, in two languages.` (English only — Substack's Arabic rendering in this field is fragile, and we'd rather lead clean.)
4. No paid tier. Category: Education.
5. Skip Notes setup if Substack prompts. Skip Recommendations setup. Decline import. Decline cross-promotion offers.

**Step 2 — Configure sections (3 minutes):**

In *Settings → Sections*, create four sections with these exact names:
- `Field notes` — original reporting and commentary (longer-form)
- `Curated` — readings of a single source
- `Letter from the editors` — once-an-issue close-the-loop note
- `عربي` — pieces published in Arabic (initially empty; signals the bilingual posture)

**Step 3 — Set the welcome email (3 minutes):**

In *Settings → Emails → Welcome email*, paste the subject and body from section 2 above. Save. (If Substack offers a preview, send one to your own inbox and confirm both the English and Arabic blocks render — particularly on iPhone, where Arabic RTL is sometimes mis-rendered by older mail clients.)

**Step 4 — Upload the wordmark (1 minute):**

*Settings → Profile → Publication logo*. Upload the wordmark (PNG export from the SVG; Substack may not accept SVG). Tagline stays the same as Step 1.

**Step 5 — Footer embed URL (1 minute):**

*Settings → Embedding*. Copy the form HTML. Paste it into a fresh message and email it to yourself, or paste it into a file in `/social-drafts/2026-06-XX-substack-embed.txt`. The Web Developer needs this string to wire the footer subscribe form in the next session. (Until that wiring lands, the only path to subscribe is via the Substack URL itself; this is acceptable for the first 24 hours.)

**Step 6 — Draft Issue 01 (8 minutes):**

1. *Dashboard → New post*. Section: `Letter from the editors`.
2. Title: `Issue 01 — Two pieces, slowly`. Subtitle: `Sierra Leone, and Bahrain.`
3. Post header image: the Sierra Leone still (1080×1080 PNG). Caption it: `Sierra Leone · سكون`.
4. Paste the body from section 3 above. Keep the links as-is — they're absolute URLs that route to the live site.
5. *Preview*. Read it on the preview page, top to bottom. Read it on a phone.
6. *Save draft*. (Do not schedule. Do not send.)

**Step 7 — Send window (3 minutes — when you're ready):**

The send window is yours. Suggested timing: **Tuesday morning Abu Dhabi, around 09:00 local.** Reasons: (a) Tuesday is a strong open-day in MENA newsletter analytics across most lists; (b) 09:00 lands while Gulf readers are at their first coffee; (c) it lets the Editor and the Manager have a final twelve-hour window to flag anything. The Friday-after-launch metrics report (return rate, open rate, replies) is on Growth.

If you'd rather send same-day, that's defensible — the publication has held the bar; restraint is not the same as anxiety.
